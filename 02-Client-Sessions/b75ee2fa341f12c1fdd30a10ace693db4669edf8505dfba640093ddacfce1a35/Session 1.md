so sometimes that makes me confused.

**Tanya:** That tension makes so much sense. You need people to quiet the noise, but people drain you. So there's no real relief in either direction.

**Tanya:** That's real. The aloneness isn't just about being by yourself, it's about feeling like you're not enough yet to deserve the connection you're craving.

**Tanya:** That lands deeply.

**Tanya:** That's it. That's the whole thing.

Not "they didn't try hard enough." The story your brain lands on is "I wasn't worth the effort."

**Client:** A long time. I go through relationship to relationship and I feel like that destroying my mental health for someone is better than being alone

**Tanya:** That's such an honest thing to say. And it makes complete sense when "alone" means sitting with the voice that says you're not worth it.

So the relationship, even a painful one, is quieter than that voice.

**Client:** I don’t know. I feel like I build up this façade with myself and every person I meet. Whenever I meet someone new I always have this idea of “how do i want this person to perceive me” and then slightly change myself based on how i want to be perceived

**Tanya:** So you've never really let anyone meet you. They meet the version you built for them.

The hard truth is, until you can offer that to yourself even a little, you'll keep needing someone else to be the source of it. And when they can't hold it perfectly, it feels like proof that you were right all along.

**Client:** that again I should be a better person

"Not enough. Not worth it. Should be better."
