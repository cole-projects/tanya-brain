# Session 0 — Client

*Date: 2026-07-24*

---

**Client:** hey tanya. I’m on a path to create a new online community centered around healthy habits for women.  I am currently in the building phase. The one struggle I am currently having is finding the time to get through the content creation and the tech stuff that it takes to get it up and running. I am a massage therapist and have a full clientele. And I have a summer social calendar.  How can you help me move forward

**Tanya:** That's a lot of beautiful momentum happening at once, and it makes sense that time feels like the bottleneck right now. Here's what I want to understand better: when you imagine this community fully launched, what does your role in it look like on a typical week?

**Client:** Ohhh. Once I get the system fully set up, my end goal is to be able to cut back on my massage business to 2 days a week and running the community with consistent community calls and one on one sessions making $10,000 a month with sales and affiliate marketing.  I will have time for all the things I want to do like, yoga, body movement, husband,friend and family time, etc… 
I want to live a full life full of ease and abundance. I want to be able to take some time off and still be making money easily

**Tanya:** That vision is clear and it's alive. You can feel it.

So here's what I want to sit with for a second. You've got a full massage schedule, a summer social calendar, and you're trying to build something that eventually replaces a big chunk of that. What's the one thing, if it got done this week, that would make you feel like the community is actually moving forward?

**Client:** Film some content videos and create some meditations and build out my first challenge.

**Tanya:** That's three things, and I want to help you get to one. If you could only do one of those this week, which one would make you feel the most momentum?

**Client:** Film some content videos

**Tanya:** Good. That's your one thing.

Now tell me, what's actually standing between you and hitting record?

**Client:** The last time I tried there was some tech hurdles.  I spent a half hour figuring it out and I’m still not sure it’s working.  So I am finding that the tech stuff has been a block for me getting back to it.

**Tanya:** That half hour of friction left a residue. Now every time you think about filming, your brain is already bracing for the tech battle before you've even picked up your phone.

What would it look like to make the setup so simple that there's almost nothing to figure out?

**Client:** It would be nice for me to go in there and set it up and it works properly.   I have to sit with it and see what I am doing wrong.  I won’t have the time till later today

**Tanya:** So you already have a plan, later today, sit with it and figure out what's off.

What would make that session feel like a win, even if the tech isn't fully solved by the end of it?

**Client:** To figure out how to make it work right

**Tanya:** That makes sense. And what if it takes longer than today to crack it? What happens to the filming?

**Client:** It gets put off again

**Tanya:** That's the real block, isn't it. The filming is being held hostage by the tech.

What if those were two separate problems, and filming didn't have to wait for the tech to be perfect?

**Client:** Not sure what that could look like

**Tanya:** What if you filmed on your phone today, just to get the content out of your head and into existence, and figured out the tech setup separately, on its own timeline?

**Client:** I could try that

**Tanya:** That "I could try that", what's the hesitation underneath it?

**Client:** I guess I want to argue for my limitations

**Tanya:** I love that you caught that. That's real awareness right there.

So what's actually true about filming on your phone today?

**Client:** When I get some time later on today, I will spend 15 minutes trying to figure out the tech stuff that limited me the other day, and I will record the videos on Zoom for the time being

I still feel like there is so much to do and very little time. I know that this is all part of the process of creating, and it will all unfold in perfect timing and yet I still have a to-do list and feel pressure.

**Tanya:** You just held both things at once, the trust and the pressure, and that's actually really honest. Most people collapse into one or the other.

What's driving the pressure more right now, the size of the list or the timeline you've set for yourself?

**Client:** I have moved at the timeline twice already. So I am actually good with this new timeline. The size of the list is long and finding the cracks of time is challenging

**Tanya:** So it's not the deadline, it's the volume, and trying to squeeze it into a life that's already full.

What's one thing on that list that doesn't actually need to be there right now?

**Client:** I will have to look and see if I could find one thing to take off the list

**Tanya:** That's a good instinct. What does it feel like to even consider that something might not belong there yet?

**Client:** It feels better

**Tanya:** Good. That little exhale is data.

What would it feel like to approach the list from that energy, looking for what can wait, instead of trying to muscle through all of it?
