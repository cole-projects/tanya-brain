**Tanya:** That's real — the fear that all that effort could be for nothing. When you think about "wasting time and effort," what does that say about you?

**Tanya:** Did you hear what you just said? You already have proof. When you've gone before, you *always* felt better going than not going, even before seeing physical results.

That shift you just made — from "what if nothing changes" to "I'm pretty sure I'd feel better" — that's your FutureYou speaking right there.

**John:** It makes me think I’m lazy

**John:** It’s likely both

**Tanya:** Fair enough. So if part of you is genuinely ready to move and part of you is genuinely resistant, what if instead of fighting the resistance, you just made it smaller?

**John:** I could go to the gym and just commit to a short workout. I’ve done that before, and it has worked to build momentum for me to want to go more and then I’ll do that for a week or two and then I’ll either get sick or hurt myself or something will come up to shift routine and then I stop going again and then I get in this mode where I’m just too lazy to go

What if every time you stop and restart isn't evidence of laziness, but just proof that you're human and life happens?

Here's the thing: you're never going to *feel* like going on day one. That heavy feeling is just part of it. The question is, are you willing to go anyway, with the resistance, instead of waiting for the resistance to go away first?
