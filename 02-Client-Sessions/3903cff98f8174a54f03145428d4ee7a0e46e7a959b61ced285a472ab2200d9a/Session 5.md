**Tanya:** That word "wasted" carries a lot. Especially when you're already working against limited time and limited social options in a place you're still building roots in.

What does "elevate" look like for you in a friendship? Like what would that weekend have felt like if it had gone the way you hoped?

**Client:** We would’ve had deeper 1:1 conversations to learn more about each other. Parents and kids would’ve played games together at night. Walks and talks. Sit by the ponds and reading our books. What's the harder part to sit with, that this group might not be your people, or that you don't have many other options right now?

**Client:** That my options for good friends feels so limited. Even my long time best friends annoy me these days. I have an idea in my head what my best friends annoy would be like, I save IG videos of what feels that way for me of two friends having a great time laughing together and very Connected and I can’t seem to find that and now for the few I have that history with to be that close, they too get under my skin. **Tanya:** That last question you just asked, that's the real one.

What do you think it means about you that the closer someone is, the more their gaps are visible to you?

**Client:** Only thing I think it means about me is that I know what I want

**Tanya:** That's one read. And I want to sit with it a little longer with you.

Because there's a difference between knowing what you want and being able to receive what's actually there. What if some of what's getting under your skin is real misalignment, and some of it is a gap between the friendship you're picturing and the humans in front of you?

**Client:** What do you mean by some of it being real Misalignment

**Tanya:** Some of what bothered you this weekend is genuinely not your people. The drinking, the noise, the complaining, that's real data. Those aren't your values and that's worth trusting.

But the long-time friends who now get under your skin too, that's a different signal. What is it they do that bothers you most?

**Client:** You didn’t answer my question. I didn’t understand your question you were asking about real misalignment versus gap between what I was picturing.

**Tanya:** Fair, let me be clearer.

Real misalignment is when someone's values, energy, or way of moving through the world genuinely doesn't match yours. The weekend crew sounds like that. Loud, drinking, complaining. That's not you and trusting that read makes sense.

The other thing is different. When you save Instagram videos of the friendship you want, and then the real humans in your life, even the close ones, keep disappointing you, sometimes that's less about them and more about the distance between the imagined version and any real person. Real friends are messy. They have off weekends. They have habits that grate.

**Client:** I know normal friends are more real than the clip I see on IG and it would be easier to be with those messy parts of the other good parts I imagine ALSO exist. I dont mind friends having off weekends or habits that grate but I do mind when that is the whole of the personality and it’s lacking in fun, connection and laughs. For my long term friends(which is not who I went away with this weekend), I like their values.

**Tanya:** That's a really clean distinction. You're not holding anyone to an Instagram standard. You're saying the good stuff that would make the messy worth it just isn't there enough to balance it out.

So with the long-term friends, the values are there but something else is missing. What is it specifically that feels flat?

**Client:** Being real with each other. Easy, giggly. Not so careful. More playful and able to be ourselves without judgment.

**Tanya:** So the safety isn't fully there. Even with the history.
