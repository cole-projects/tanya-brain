# Session 9 — Client

*Date: 2026-07-21*

---

**Client:** Hey Tanya

**Tanya:** So glad you're back. What's on your mind today?

**Client:** Hey Tanya

**Tanya:** Hey, good to hear from you. What are you bringing today?

**Client:** I don’t even know where to start. I love my partner but lately I feel like I’m keeping score without meaning to like who did dishes, who handled the kids morning and who remembered the appt. Its exhausting and I don’t know when that feeling started

**Tanya:** That kind of invisible tally is one of the most draining things to carry, especially because you're not doing it on purpose.

What do you think the scoreboard is really trying to tell you?

**Client:** Honestly I think it’s me trying to feel like it’s fair. Like if I keep track at least I’m not the only one who notices what’s not getting done but I don’t think k I’ve ever said this to my partner

**Tanya:** So the scoreboard is less about blame and more about not wanting to disappear into the invisible work.

What's made it hard to say that out loud to them?

**Client:** I think part of it is that there’s never time because I’m always busy with kids and then too tired at end of the night. I’m also scared it’ll come out like I’m complaining and saying he doesn’t do enough. I don’t feel like we are a team and it feels lonely

**Tanya:** That word, lonely. That's the real thing underneath all of it, isn't it.

What would feeling like a team actually look like for you?

**Client:** Hmmm. Being a team feels like him noticing what needs to be done before I have to ask or maybe checking in with me

**Tanya:** So it's not just the doing. It's the being seen, him noticing without you having to flag it first.

What would it mean to you if he did that?

**Client:** It would feel like he gets me and that would be great but I’m not sure how to approach it in a way that will come across well. What should I do

**Tanya:** That longing to be gotten, really seen by the person closest to you, that's not a small thing.

And I want to sit with that for a second before we go anywhere practical, because I think that's where the real weight is. When you imagine actually having that conversation with him, what feels most impossible about it?

**Client:** That he’ll get defensive

**Tanya:** That fear makes a lot of sense given what you've been holding.

What do you know about him that tells you defensiveness would be his first move?

**Client:** Past conversations where he’s said he is doing more than I give him credit for and then he retreats

**Tanya:** So you've seen that pattern before. He feels unseen too, and then you both end up further apart than when you started.

What if the conversation wasn't about the scoreboard at all, but about the loneliness?
