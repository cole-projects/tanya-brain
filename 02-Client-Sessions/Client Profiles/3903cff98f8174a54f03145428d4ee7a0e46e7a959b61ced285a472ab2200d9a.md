*What's really underneath the surface problem.*

- The motivation gap may not be a discipline problem — it may be a power or worth problem. When someone can't move toward something they say they want, it often signals a hidden conflict (fear of failure, belief it won't work, or that they don't deserve the outcome).

- Session 2 adds a layer: the client's attachment to the teaching/telling role in their coaching practice mirrors something about their relationship to competence and identity. They are most comfortable in positions of mastery. The discomfort with coaching through questions — knowing the answer and having to hold it — may echo how they relate to their own stuck places. Possible through-line: client struggles to move toward what they want (exercise/health) AND struggles to tolerate not immediately resolving what they can see (in clients). Both involve sitting with discomfort when action or resolution is available.
