# Client Profile — Joel

> This file is the memory layer for Joel. Update after every session.
> Claude reads this before responding to ensure continuity across conversations.

---

## Core Wound
- **Primary:** Not Safe
- **Secondary:** Not Supported / Not Powerful
- **Belief underneath:** "I gave up my entire identity to make the marriage work and it still failed. I can't trust that taking care of myself will be okay. I have to control and figure everything out to feel safe."

---

## What They Came In With
- Recently divorced after 15 years of marriage
- Lost 100 pounds in 7 months (major physical transformation that opened emotional work)
- Moved back to his 80 acres in Oklahoma after living in Chicago suburbs
- Wants $50K/month income
- Feels lost but is trying to find direction

---

## The Deeper Issue
- Deep identity crisis — became someone he didn't want to be while trying to fit into his ex-wife's life
- Shame about the marriage failure (*"She felt like she'd done it all alone"*)
- Significant emotional and body work done, but still learning to integrate it into daily thinking
- Believes he has to figure out every detail to feel safe
- Hardest on himself — his own worst critic (only his father has been harder on him)

---

## Recurring Patterns
- Perfectionism disguised as high standards
- Gets stuck in negative thought loops that feel like "the only truth"
- Wants to figure things out alone rather than let support in
- Old narrative: *"I don't feel I have choice in how I'm feeling about it"*
- But is learning: the opposite direction is always equally available

## Key Breakthroughs
- Realized the marriage served a purpose: *"You rubbed each other in the right way to get set on a track that will change things forever"*
- Understood his body transformation required emotional work — can't force it without a mindset shift
- Got that "I'm lost but pointing in a direction" is actually momentum
- Beginning to trust the mechanics of how the universe works
- Sees that circumstances don't cause feelings — thoughts about circumstances do

---

## Still Stuck On
- Trusting that the opposite (positive) direction is as easy to reach as the negative spiral
- Not overthinking the rewrite process (*"I just plain suck at rewrites"*)
- Releasing the need to control and figure out everything
- Fully believing he's enough without proving it through money or achievement
- Sitting with uncertainty without immediately needing answers

---

## Their Language
- *"I'm still lost, but at least I know I'm pointing a direction"*
- *"Moving through it"* (vs. getting rid of it)
- *"I'm my own worst critic"*
- *"Reality sets in"*
- *"The mechanics of how things work"*

## What They Respond Well To
- Logical explanation of mechanics (neural pathways, law of opposites, belief chain)
- Permission to be a learner — doesn't need to be perfect at rewrites yet
- Big-picture reframes about purpose in painful experiences
- Direct coaching on the belief → thought → feeling → action chain
- Separating the circumstance from the story he tells about it
- Real examples (Tanya's story about the million in debt)
- Building trust through understanding, not pressure
