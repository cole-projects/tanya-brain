# Client Profile — Brooke

> This file is the memory layer for Brooke. Update after every session.
> Claude reads this before responding to ensure continuity across conversations.

---

## Core Wound
- **Primary:** Not Enough
- **Secondary:** Not Worthy
- **Belief underneath:** "If I don't do everything myself and prove my worth through work, I'm not valuable. I don't deserve to receive without earning it first."

---

## What They Came In With
- Running a nonprofit (8 years old, in 5 schools, expanding to 10+)
- Organization couldn't pay her for 2 months — money stress
- Fear that it "won't work for me" even as things are clearly working
- New major opportunity: meeting about expanding to 21 schools
- Trying to keep up with energy work while "too busy"

---

## The Deeper Issue
- Imposter syndrome disguised as abundance mindset — can see the vision but can't fully believe it's for her
- Deep scarcity wound around money: equates worth with income
- Habit of narrating how expensive everything is (Bali, Vegas gymnastics) — keeps scarcity alive
- Shames herself when she notices lack thoughts (*"shut up, get over it"*)
- Doesn't fully allow herself to rest or receive without justification

---

## Recurring Patterns
- Sees real evidence of success but can't quite claim it for herself
- Catches herself in "everything is so expensive" narration, then shames herself for it
- Oscillates between "I can manifest $2M!" and "everything costs too much" 
- Over-functions (yoga teaching + nonprofit + grant applications simultaneously)
- Waits for external validation rather than embodying worthiness
- The pattern: excitement about expansion → immediate fear it won't work → back to excitement

## Key Breakthroughs
- Realized she doesn't need to manifest 248 schools — just knowing it's *possible* eases the scarcity
- Saw that making any plan for money stabilizes her nervous system more than trying to manifest
- Got that having momentum means she's doing the work correctly
- Recognized her "expensive things" narration is old-train thinking, not truth
- Understood the 7-year anniversary of her nonprofit is massive proof of her capability
- Began catching imposter syndrome thoughts and rewriting them rather than shaming herself

---

## Still Stuck On
- Fully believing she deserves consistent income (paying herself properly)
- Stopping the "things are so expensive" loop — it's addictive and she knows it
- Separating excitement about expansion from fear it won't work
- Allowing success without needing to prove she earned it
- Resting without guilt (booked Bali trip, then felt guilty about cost and time)

---

## Their Language
- *"There's so much positive motion happening"*
- *"Fear that it's not going to work for me"*
- *"The devil near the door"* (brain fighting back when she's close to change)
- *"I feel the momentum"*
- *"I've made a career out of talking about how expensive things are"*
- *"I'm already trying"*

---

## What They Respond Well To
- Celebrating visible evidence of what's already working
- Old train / new train framing (gentle, not shaming)
- Permission to feel both scared AND excited at the same time
- Practical frameworks first (stabilize → optimize; plan → feeling follows)
- Catching her patterns with humor, not judgment
- Teaching her that awareness is the breakthrough — not forcing change
- Long-form coaching (she needs context and nuance, not just short answers)

---

## Where They Are in Their Journey
**Mid-stage** (~3 months). In the paradox of "it's working AND I'm terrified it won't." Has good self-awareness, using the tools, but scarcity beliefs are still wired in the nervous system even as evidence of abundance grows. The money wound specifically needs continued work.
