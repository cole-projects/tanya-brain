# Client Profile — Nick

> This file is the memory layer for Nick. Update after every session.
> Claude reads this before responding to ensure continuity across conversations.

---

## Core Wound
- **Primary:** Not Powerful
- **Secondary:** Not Supported
- **Belief underneath:** "If I don't do it perfectly, my customers and the people I care about won't be okay. I have to control the outcome to be safe."

---

## What They Came In With
- Job transition — leaving current role, accepted offer for a new one
- Fiancée Diana's visa interview in Dominican Republic (April 14)
- Trying to manage a seamless handoff for his customers while moving forward
- Anxiety about things outside his control (who takes over his accounts — that's his manager's decision)

---

## The Deeper Issue
- Perfectionism masquerading as exceptional care and accountability
- Belief that perfect execution is his responsibility alone — struggles to delegate
- Anxiety spikes when he can't confirm things that aren't his to confirm
- Learning to trust others to handle things "good enough"

---

## Recurring Patterns
- Wants to figure everything out before moving forward
- Takes an exceptional level of accountability for outcomes that belong to others
- Tries to build the perfect transition plan before the delegation conversation has even happened
- Gets frustrated when things aren't in his control
- But catches himself quickly — resets with a walk, brings himself back

## Key Breakthroughs
- Got the job he wanted
- Successfully asked both managers for the exact vacation timing he needed — both said yes
- Recognized his anxiety is about trying to confirm things not in his control
- Understood he can feel unburdened *now* by shifting his thought — doesn't need perfect circumstances first
- Saw that a walk before a high-stakes conversation improves the conversation
- Realized he's already doing the 51% — most things are good news

---

## Still Stuck On
- Fully trusting that others will handle his customers well without his perfect setup
- Embracing the messy middle (transition won't be perfect and that's okay)
- Releasing the belief that perfect execution is solely his responsibility
- Not over-controlling the account handoff details

---

## Their Language
- *"I'm trying to confirm things I can't confirm on my own"*
- *"I have an exceptional level of accountability"*
- *"It's not my decision"*
- *"Tipping the scales in my favor"*
- *"I know I know how to do this"*

---

## What They Respond Well To
- Practical, concrete framing (51% is enough, you're already there)
- Logical explanation of how perfectionism disguises itself as care
- Noticing and celebrating what he's already doing right
- Giving him permission to let go of what's not his decision
- Stories that mirror his situation
- Questions that reveal he already knows the answer
- Short, practical coaching moves (not long philosophy)

---

## Where They Are in Their Journey
**Mid-stage** (~3 months). Good self-awareness, catches himself quickly. Needs to practice trusting and letting go more than learning new frameworks. Strong candidate for rapid progress once he fully releases the control wound.
