# Client Profile — JC

> This file is the memory layer for this client. Update it after every session.
> Claude reads this before responding to ensure continuity across conversations.

---

## Core Wound
*Which of the Five Wounds is dominant?*

- **Primary:** Not Powerful (stuck at the edge, paralysis about leaping, doesn't trust she can figure things out or handle challenges)
- **Secondary:** Not Supported (external voices louder than inner compass; opinions override inner knowing)
- **Tertiary:** Not Safe (overwhelm affecting basic functioning, fear of everything falling apart, conflict avoidance)
- **Belief underneath:** "If I try and fail, it will expose that I was fooling herself and prove I'm not capable"

---

## What They Came In With
*The surface presenting problem when they first started.*

- Feeling stuck and unable to move toward what she wants
- Paralyzed about leaving her job to start her own business (idea she's carried for 2 years)

---

## The Deeper Issue
*What's really underneath the surface problem.*

- Fundamental lack of trust in her own resourcefulness and ability to figure things out
- Terror that trying and failing would expose her as "naïve" and force her back to old life with everyone knowing she failed
- Nervous system doesn't have enough evidence yet that she can handle challenges
- Losing connection to her core motivation in the noise of external opinions and logistics
- End-of-life-phase overwhelm causing system overload and regression to basic survival mode
- Complete protective shutdown during overwhelm periods - unable to access support even when offered
- **Complete connection/intimacy intolerance:** Cannot tolerate even greeting-level human connection or basic inquiry without triggering protective shutdown

---

## Recurring Patterns
*What keeps coming up session after session.*

- Standing at the edge but unable to leap (diving board metaphor)
- All-or-nothing thinking: wild success or devastating failure
- Intellectually understanding concepts but not trusting them "in her bones"
- Letting external voices (friends, doubts) become louder than her internal compass
- Getting lost in logistics and losing sight of core motivation
- When stressed or activated, even simple problem-solving becomes difficult (food, basic needs)
- **End-of-phase overwhelm:** When major transitions approach, everything feels like it's "piling up at once"
- **Complete protective withdrawal pattern:** During sustained overwhelm, cannot access any support and immediately disconnects from all contact
- **Immediate session termination ritual:** Work stress, interpersonal conflict, or general overwhelm triggers instant "end session" response
- **Brief positive share followed by immediate exit:** Pattern of sharing good news then immediately ending session - unable to stay present for positive recognition
- **Sustained shutdown periods:** Extended cycles where system cannot engage meaningfully for weeks
- **Zero tolerance for any form of connection:** Even basic greeting responses or casual check-ins trigger immediate protective termination - no engagement threshold at all
- **Confusion about coaching language/process:** Protective responses to coaching terminology or check-in questions that feel foreign or invasive

- Realized coaching is for "recalibration" not roadmaps - staying connected to what matters when things get noisy

## What They Respond Well To
*Coaching moves that land for this client.*

- Body awareness questions ("how does that land in your body?")
- Naming what's real vs. offering reframes
- Helping her find her own solutions rather than being given advice
- Both/and thinking vs. either/or
- Small, concrete next steps she can't talk herself out of
- Recognition of shifts she's already making
- Permission to not have all the answers
- Helping her distinguish between external noise and internal compass
- Reflecting back the magnitude of shifts she's making
- Simple acknowledgment and space to share progress
- Reframing apparent setbacks as integration and consolidation
- Brief check-ins that honor her autonomy
- Acknowledging her appreciation and positive feedback
- Offering practical options first when in stress mode
- Getting underneath surface problems to the real issue
- Reframing business ideas as legacy projects that honor deeper values
- Celebrating alignment and connection to source
- Staying out of her way when she's in flow and action
- **Immediate session closure without questions or exploration when she requests it**
- **Consistent warm presence statement without pressure ("When you're here, I'm here")**
- **Complete acceptance of protective withdrawal without trying to understand or fix**
- **Zero pressure to explain or process when in shutdown mode**
- **Heart emoji presence that maintains connection without demands**
- **Brief acknowledgment of positive outcomes without pushing for deeper exploration**
- **Clarifying coaching language when it triggers confusion or protective responses**
