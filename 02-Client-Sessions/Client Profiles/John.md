# Client Profile — John

> This file is the memory layer for this client. Update it after every session.
> Claude reads this before responding to ensure continuity across conversations.

---

## Core Wound
*Which of the Five Wounds is dominant?*

- **Primary:** Not Worthy
- **Secondary:** Not Powerful
- **Belief underneath:** "I'm lazy" / "What if all my effort is wasted?"

---

## What They Came In With
*The surface presenting problem when they first started.*

- Wants to get healthier and summer body ready, joined a gym but not going despite having time and desire

---

## The Deeper Issue
*What's really underneath the surface problem.*

- Fear of wasting time and effort if results don't meet expectations; carrying "I'm lazy" story that gets reinforced every time he doesn't follow through; using Hurley's separation anxiety as protection from risking disappointment

---

## Recurring Patterns
*What keeps coming up session after session.*

- Builds momentum for 1-2 weeks, something external happens (sick, routine shift), stops going, calls himself lazy instead of just restarting
- All-or-nothing thinking about results ("only worth it if I'm happy with how I look and feel")
- Using dog's separation anxiety as reason to avoid leaving house
- Pattern of scheduling sessions then not showing up or engaging minimally

## Key Breakthroughs
*Moments where something genuinely shifted.*

- Recognized he has proof that going always feels better than not going
- Shifted from "I'm lazy" to "it feels empowering" when imagining showing up
- Saw the pattern isn't laziness but normal human stops/starts plus external events

---

## Still Stuck On
*What hasn't fully resolved yet — important for next session.*

- Hurley's separation anxiety as barrier to leaving house (wants dog to be happy, not just managing)
- Building routine that works for both him and dog
- Deep fear of effort being wasted
- Consistent engagement with coaching process itself

## What They Respond Well To
*Coaching moves that land for this client.*

- Having his feelings normalized ("That makes complete sense")
- Being shown his own contradictions/proof
- Making commitments smaller and more doable
- Planning for obstacles in advance
- Separating his worth from outcomes
- Direct, warm reconnection when he shows up
