## Core Wound
*Which of the Five Wounds is dominant?*

- **Belief underneath:** "If I try my hardest and it still doesn't work, that proves I was never enough to begin with — so it's safer not to fully try." Underneath that: "I am not worth changing for." ## The Deeper Issue
*What's really underneath the surface problem.*

- Aloneness is unbearable because it means being alone with an inner voice that says "you are not enough and not worth it." Relationships — even painful ones — temporarily silence that voice. The search for a partner is not really about love; it is about outsourcing the answer to the question "am I enough?"
- They have never let anyone meet the real version of themselves. They build a persona tailored to how they want to be perceived, which means even when someone chooses them, some part of them knows the real self was never chosen — so the void remains.
- Self-protection masquerades as laziness: if they never fully try, they never have to find out that trying wasn't enough either.

---

## Recurring Patterns
*What keeps coming up session after session.*

- Seeking external validation (romantic partners, friends) as the primary source of worth
- Building a curated façade for every new person they meet; never showing the unfiltered self
- Over-functioning in relationships to make them work, then blaming the other person when it fails — while privately landing on "I wasn't worth changing for"
- Avoiding solitude through dating apps, weed, and constant mental noise
- Self-sabotage via avoidance (making excuses to skip work or social invitations) rooted in fear that full effort + failure = proof of unworthiness
- The "should be better" voice activates the moment they are alone — traced back to a critical older sister in childhood
