## Core Wound
*Which of the Five Wounds is dominant?*

*Primary and Secondary must use only these exact labels: **Not Worthy**, **Not Enough**, **Not Supported**, **Not Powerful**, **Not Safe**. Do not invent custom wound names.*

- **Primary:** Not Enough
- **Secondary:** Not Safe
- **Belief underneath:** What I make with my own hands might not be good enough — not clean enough, not professional enough, not credible enough — to be taken seriously by others. The homemade nature of the thing is both its heart and its liability, and that tension hasn't resolved yet.

---

## What They Came In With
*The surface presenting problem when they first started.*

- Wants to build a family business with her 9-year-old daughter Myla that is mobile, creative, and meaningful — initially framed as a request for business strategy and product direction.

---

## The Deeper Issue
*What's really underneath the surface problem.*

- She has lived the exhaustion of trying to feed her family well on a tight budget while also being present with her daughter. The business is an attempt to solve the problem she personally experienced — and underneath the strategy questions is a woman who isn't yet sure that what she creates from her own life and hands is worthy of other people's trust and money.
- The packaging and "cleanliness" concern that emerged by Session 1 is not really about packaging. It's about whether *she* — and what she makes — is safe to be seen.

---

## Recurring Patterns
*What keeps coming up session after session.*

- Oscillates between expansive vision (multiple trailers, a partner, a French farmhouse brand) and grounding doubt (will it actually sell, will it look clean, will people trust it).
- Jumps between big-picture dreaming and granular execution concerns without a stable middle — the "how do I do the next thing" layer is shaky.
- Seeks external validation before trusting her own instinct (asking what group would buy, what Bend expects nutritionally, whether a jar feels safe vs. a sealed package).
