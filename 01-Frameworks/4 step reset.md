# 4 Step Reset

## Step 1 — Feel Your Now Feeling

- Name it in one word
- Describe how it feels in your body and what it's like feeling that way

You can't be in the emotion when you observe it. In order to go in and describe how it feels, you have to go into your outer awareness, your watcher, to observe yourself. And in order to observe yourself feeling, you have to take yourself out of the feeling temporarily in order to observe yourself feeling the feeling. In order to observe the feeling you cannot be in the feeling.

If you say, "I don't know," then you block yourself from that awareness and from that power vantage point not enmeshed in the emotion.

So in order to describe the feeling, you have to switch back and forth in your body from being the awareness of it to being in your body in it. Invites you to be present with the emotion and notice involuntary physiological sensations in your body. E.g. hunger, sickness, coldness, fullness. These happen in your body and travel to your mind.

## Step 2 — Find the Thought Causing the Feeling

All feelings are caused by thoughts and thoughts are just sentences in our mind.

Emotions aren't caused by things happening outside of you. They are caused by the meaning you attribute to it through the thoughts that you think about them — but the thing you're thinking about does not carry inherent meaning. Emotions require consciousness and start in mind and travel to your body. Things outside of you can be concentrations, but they cannot cause emotions.

Find the sentence causing the emotion. Not a thought attributed to a circumstance causing it.

What is your thought about the neutral circumstance?

The economy, Covid — in and of itself doesn't have the ability to scare us unless we have a thought about it. Otherwise we wouldn't have different people having different feelings, and if the circumstance was responsible for how we all felt then we would all feel the same thing. And the reason we all feel differently about it is because we have different sentences in our mind.

Find the sentence that is causing you to feel the way you feel.

You'll realize you are very powerful over your emotional life. Make the connection between the way you feel and the sentence in your mind.

Wait a minute. What sentences are being fed to me from my brain? Important to know because it's causing how you feel. And if you aren't aware of it you can't change it. And you'll feel powerless.

Our brains are like toddlers with knives.

Every thought you think is a choice. If you don't decide deliberately what to think, your brain will do it for you unsupervised.

You can't control the world. You can control what you think.

## Step 3 — Create a New and True Thought and Give It Equal Air Time

Not asking you to give up your belief of XYZ. But asking you to consider that also…

Just introduce a new one and feel both.

Find new truths that produce a new feeling.

**Example:**
- "I can't get it all done today."
- "I'm going to get much of it done today."

Can feel some sense of accomplishment alongside your sense of inadequacy. Equally true, a little more neutral, and allows for a new emotion. Holding space for both.

Introduce equally believable ideas that balance out that emotion and you will start to feel better.

This:
- Proves your thoughts cause your feelings
- Brings you into awareness and has you paying attention to yourself
- Makes choices on how to think and directs your own awareness with deliberate creation
- Helps you learn how to create new emotion in a repeatable way
