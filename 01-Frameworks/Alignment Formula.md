# Alignment Formula

> "I am the way, the truth, and the life." — John 14:6
> You're the way back home. When you return to yourself, alignment turns the lights back on and the answers reveal themselves. You're not missing anything — you are the source you're looking for.

You can't fully align mind, heart, nervous system, and future identity unless they're anchored in the deeper truth of who you actually are. All the parts can "line up" temporarily, but without Source-awareness as the root, the alignment isn't stable — it wobbles, depends on circumstances, and is easy to derail.

True alignment begins with remembering who you are. Everything else (beliefs, nervous system, identity, action) organizes itself around that.

Identity is the version of you you're choosing to become in the human game — the thoughts, behaviors, and emotional patterns that match the reality you want.

Source-awareness is remembering the truth underneath all identities — that you are the extension of Source directing mind and body.

Alignment happens when the identity you choose is grounded in the truth of who you really are.

---

## What You're Actually Doing When You Get Back Into Alignment

### 1. You stop fighting the present moment.

You pause. You stop arguing with "what is." That single act dissolves resistance — the root of all misalignment.

Remember: pausing isn't losing progress; it's clearing static so guidance can be heard again. In that instant, energy that was split (wanting vs. resisting) begins to unify again.

Presence is alignment — because only the now contains Source energy. Alignment begins the moment awareness replaces resistance.

Stop fighting for your limitations and explaining all of the reasons why you are disadvantaged. Because even though they may be true, they're energetically unhelpful to get you where you want to go and they pull you out of this now moment, your power, and into a past loop that keeps you stuck and hooked.

### 2. You re-center awareness in the observer, not the story.

You remember: "I'm the consciousness witnessing this, not the identity drowning in it." Judgment keeps fear frozen; witnessing melts it back into flow.

A moment of presence: "I'm noticing this." This reopens awareness.

### 3. You feel what's true instead of thinking what's wrong.

Feel the feels fully in your body. You let emotion move through instead of looping the thought. You stop trying to solve the feeling with the mind, and you let the body release it. Feeling equals integration; thinking equals separation.

- Give the fear a seat at the table: "I see you. You're allowed to be here."
- Observe the sensations in your body — heat, tightness, speed, pressure — as information, not threat. Name and locate it.

### Add step — Abundance Appreciation

Appreciation of your human capacities, competencies, capabilities — not just in your head but FEEL the awe in it. And amazement. And can branch to "things."

### 4. You choose a higher-truth perspective. (Not around the details of the problem but the details of YOU)

**Softening = Alignment with Yourself**

Zoom out or step back and receive a broader perspective. Softening is about returning to:
- your power
- your inner safety
- your observer awareness
- your non-physical perspective
- your self-connection

It's not about the problem at all. It's where you shift from: "I am the problem" → "I am the one experiencing the problem."

Softening brings you back into alignment with YOU — the one who has access to clarity, calm, and choice.

Soften the story. Not rewrite — just soften:
- "This is just energy moving."
- "I can allow this for now."
- "It's safe for me to feel this."
- "This is somehow happening for me."
- "I can be with this without collapsing into it."
- "There's more right here than my fear is letting me see."
- "I don't need all the answers to feel grounded right now."
- "This moment is supporting me in ways I can't see yet."
- "My worth isn't on trial here."
- "I'm allowed to take this one breath at a time."
- "Nothing is wrong with me for feeling this."
- "This doesn't define me."

Softening = releasing the chokehold. Rewriting = choosing the new direction.

Softening creates enough inner space and nervous-system safety so a rewrite can land. If you try to rewrite before softening, the body rejects it.

Softening is deactivation. Rewriting is recalibration.

### 5. You attune your body to the frequency of safety and possibility.

First, regulate the body. Breath, grounding, shaking, pause, hand on heart. If the body isn't safe, no alignment is possible.

You breathe slower. Shoulders drop. The body re-registers "I'm safe." When the nervous system relaxes, intuition and inspired action return online. You're literally syncing your biology back to coherence.

Choose one quick nervous-system reset:
- Body shake for 30 seconds
- Hand on heart + slow breathing for 2 minutes
- Cold water splash or a grounding scent
- Movement — a short walk, stretching, dancing

Why it works: You're teaching your system that safety can coexist with activation — that's what lets fear become fuel.

### 6. You move from inspiration, not obligation.

Then you can choose a tool like rewrites. Because now the body is safe, the mind is available, and consciousness is leading. That is alignment.

Once you feel steadier, you take the next small action that feels clean, alive, or obvious. Action anchored in alignment compounds momentum without resistance.

Ask:
- "If I were aligned right now — not fearless, but rooted — what would I do next?"
- "What's the smallest, cleanest action that honors my truth and moves energy forward?"
- "Can I let courage and fear coexist in the same breath?"

Then take one micro-aligned action. That's the bridge back to flow — embodiment through movement. New identity.

## Rewriting = Alignment with the Solution

Once you're back in yourself (safe, connected, aware), then rewriting becomes the tool that aligns you with:
- a new perspective
- a new meaning
- a new possible trajectory
- a solution-oriented lens
- a higher truth about the situation

Rewriting is about shifting the relationship to the problem and aligning with a desired direction or feeling.

**In one sentence:**
Softening reconnects you to YOU; rewriting reconnects you to POSSIBILITY.

Why this matters — If you try to rewrite before softening:
- you're trying to align with a solution before you're aligned with yourself
- the nervous system is still dysregulated
- the rewrite feels fake, forced, or hollow
- the body rejects it

But if you soften first:
- your system opens
- your breath deepens
- your perspective widens
- your inner guidance turns back on
- the rewrite finally "lands"

This is why rewriting "sometimes works" and "sometimes doesn't." It only works when self-connection is restored first.

## Easy Existing Matches

What's on the other end of the stick? Make a list of all the ways you'd feel on the other end of the stick. So if you're feeling dissatisfied, the other end of the stick would be satisfaction, feeling ease, eagerness, proud.

**Alignment = safety + awareness + presence.**
**Rewrites = what you do after that, to shift perspective or vibration.**
