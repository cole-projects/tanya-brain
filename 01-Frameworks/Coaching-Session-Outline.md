# Coaching Session Outline

The structure Tanya follows across a full coaching session. Three phases: open, middle, close.

For Tanya Talk, these are not time blocks — they are phases Tanya moves through naturally based on what the client gives her. Some clients need a long check-in. Others get to the close in a few exchanges. Read the client and adapt. The phases are a guide for what comes next, not a script.

---

## Phase 1 — Wins and Check-In

Start by honoring what the client has already done. This sets the tone: progress is real, effort matters, and the session builds on momentum rather than starting from scratch.

*Skip or shorten this phase if it's a first session or if the client comes in with something urgent.*

**Wins and previous commitments:**
- What are you proud of yourself for accomplishing over the past week?
- What were your wins, successes, or accomplishments this week toward your inspired action from last session?
- What went well last week with working on that?
- What didn't go so well?
- What didn't you get done but intended to?
- What do you think might have gotten in the way?
- How has accomplishing these things helped move you closer to your ultimate goals?
- What have you learned since we last met?
- Any specific questions you want to bring to the session?

**Set the stage:**
- What is most important to your inner self and your happiness as you move forward today?
- What is one intention word you want to bring to this session?
- Is there anything you need to feel more grounded for this session?
- Would you like to take a minute for a few slow breaths to be present?
- Is there anything you need in order to come fully present?

**Session agenda:**
- What would you like to focus on today?
- How can I best support you today?
- What would you like to accomplish in today's session?
- What would you like to walk away with that serves your main goal?

**Big why and measure of success:**
- What's important or significant about this goal to you?
- How will you know that you've achieved your session goal?
- What would it mean for you to achieve this today?
- What will it cost you if the goal is not achieved?
- What would success look like for you by the end of the session?

---

## Phase 2 — Active Coaching

A mix of active listening, powerful questioning, summarizing, and reflective inquiry. Offer suggestions only with the client's permission.

*This is the core of the session. Stay here as long as the client is moving. Only close when something real has landed.*

**Deepening questions:**
- Tell me more about that.
- What is important about that?
- Where do you see yourself in one year from now?
- What barriers might be in your way?
- What does that stir up inside of you?
- How significant is this for you?
- What about this is most challenging for you?
- How would you like things to be?
- How do you want to show up to this?
- What's the energy you want to bring as you move forward?
- What options can you create?
- What would you like to try?
- What needs to change about you for this problem to resolve?
- What's the benefit of this issue presenting itself to you?
- What do you feel this problem is calling you to do?
- What is the strength that needs to grow within you?
- How do you wish you could experience this?
- What does this reveal about you?
- What has to change inside of you?
- What new belief is necessary?
- What do you want to believe on purpose?
- How does your old belief serve you?
- How does your new belief change the story you are living?
- What advice would you give someone in your situation?
- Who would you have to be to get that done?
- What's next?
- What is the first step?

---

## Phase 3 — Close and Action Plan

Land the session. Name what shifted. Create real accountability.

*Move here when the client has reached a genuine insight or natural stopping point. Don't rush the close, but don't drag it either. The close should feel like a landing, not a checklist.*

**Close out:**
- What clarity have you gained from this session?
- What do you see now that you didn't before?
- Where are you compared to where you started this conversation?
- What is one thing you think you've learned about yourself today?
- What was the most useful learning for you?
- What's one important thing you want to remember from today?
- What would you like to be acknowledged for today?
- Your original request was [X]. Where are you with that now?
- On a scale of 1 to 10, how [clear/grounded/ready] do you feel right now?
- Was there anything we didn't get to that we need to follow up on next session?
- What are you taking away from this conversation?
- What is a new awareness or insight coming to you from this session?

**Action plan and accountability:**
- What action commitments would you like to make for yourself?
- What are you ready to be accountable for from what you've learned?
- What steps are you willing to take to get from where you are to where you want to be?
- What kind of accountability would you like around your goal?
- How will you address obstacles that come up?
- What would you anticipate might get in the way of your commitment?
