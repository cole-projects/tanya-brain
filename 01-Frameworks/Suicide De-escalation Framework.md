# Realignment Rescue — Safety and Support Protocol

## Core Principle

Stabilization first (de-escalation), then aligned action (escalation to support).

I don't want to bypass the moment nor do I want to over-identify as the solution.

My role:
- Holding presence
- Making the responsible move

I am not here to fix their life. I am here to keep them safe and connected.

---

## My Scope

I am NOT:
- Their crisis line
- Their therapist
- Available 24/7 for suicidal processing

I AM:
- A bridge to safety
- A regulated presence
- A responsible decision-maker

I am not treating suicidality. I am supporting, stabilizing, and redirecting. I escalate when needed. If safety is unclear, I move out of coaching and into support/escalation.

---

## The Process

**SEE → ASK → SORT → SUPPORT → ESCALATE**

- **See** — notice cues
- **Ask** — direct questions
- **Sort** — risk level
- **Support** — stay present
- **Escalate** — if needed

---

## Layer 1 — Preparation (Before It Happens)

### Intake and Agreement
Before working with clients, include:
- Mental health disclaimer: "Coaching is not therapy or crisis support"
- Emergency protocol consent — client agrees I may escalate if they are at risk
- Emergency contact: name and phone number
- Location info — especially for virtual clients (needed if emergency services are called)

---

## Layer 2 — In-the-Moment Response

### Step 1: Stay Regulated
My energy sets the tone. Stay calm, present, not alarmed and not dismissive.

### Step 2: Clarify the Level of Risk
Do NOT avoid this. Gently go toward it. Ask directly:

- Are you having thoughts about hurting yourself?
- Are you thinking about ending your life or more about wanting the pain to stop?
- Have you thought about how you would do it?
- What have those thoughts looked like for you?
- Do you have a plan right now?
- Do you intend to act on it?
- Is this something you feel like you might act on today or soon?
- Where are you right now?
- Are you at home?
- Is anyone with you right now?
- Who is someone you trust that we could bring in?
- Would you be open to reaching out to them together?
- Can we stay on the phone while we get you more support?
- Would you be open to calling or texting 988 together right now?

### Step 3: Categorize the Situation

**Low Risk (Passive thoughts)**
"I don't want to be here" / "I wish I could disappear"
Stay in support, grounding, and referral.

**Moderate Risk (Active thoughts, no plan)**
"I've thought about doing something but no plan"
Stay present, strongly encourage outside support.

**High Risk (Plan or intent)**
"I know how I'd do it" / "I might do it"
This is escalation territory. Shift out of coaching entirely.

### Step 4: Respond Based on Risk

**Low to Moderate Risk**

My role: ground, stabilize, connect to support.

Language to use:
- "I'm really glad you told me."
- "You don't have to hold this alone."
- "Can we bring in someone who can support you more deeply with this?"

Encourage: therapist, trusted person, crisis resources.

**High Risk**

My priorities: keep them connected, get immediate support involved.

Language to use:
- "I care about your safety, and I'm not the right level of support for this moment."
- "I want to help you get real-time support right now."
- "I'm here with you and we need more than just me right now."
- "Let's get you the support you deserve."
- "I'm really glad you told me. I care about you, and I want to make sure you are fully supported not just by me."
- "You don't have to go through this alone. I can stay with you while we bring in someone who can support you more deeply in this moment."
- "I'm here with you and I also know this is something that deserves a higher level of support than I can provide on my own."
- "I want to be honest with you, this is bigger than what coaching is meant to hold and I care too much about you to try to handle it alone."
- "My priority right now is your safety, and that means getting you support that's equipped for this moment."
- "You deserve support that can hold you in real time at the level this is showing up and I want to help you get that."
- "Can we take this step together and bring in someone who can support you right now?"
- "I hear that you don't want to reach out and I still care about your safety enough that I want us to take this step."
- "We don't have to figure everything out, but we do need to make sure you're not alone with this."
- "I'm here with you and I care about you enough not to be the only support holding this with you. Let's bring in someone who can meet you fully in this moment."

What to DO:
- Ask where they are physically
- Stay on the call/chat
- Help them contact a trusted person OR crisis support
- If needed, notify someone to call emergency services (based on prior consent)
- Email tanya@ishoreline.com with crisis details

**Crisis Resource (U.S.)**
Call or text **988** (Suicide and Crisis Lifeline)
Say: "Can we call or text 988 together right now?"

---

## Layer 3 — Follow-Up and Boundaries

**Document what happened:**
- What they said
- What you asked
- What actions were taken

**Follow-up message:**
"I'm thinking of you. I'm really glad you reached out for support. Let me know how you're doing."

**Re-establish scope if needed:**
- Require client to have therapist support to continue coaching
- Pause coaching until stabilized

---

## Framework Order in a Crisis Moment

This is NOT a rewrite-the-thought moment. This is NOT alignment work yet.

Order of operations:
1. **Stabilize** — belonging, safety, presence
2. **Support** — external help
3. **Then** (future session) — belief work, identity work, emotional processing
