# FYF Energy Check

🌟 Living in Integrity with Your Choices
1. Definition of Energy Work
Energy work isn’t just meditation, journaling, or a morning routine. It’s any moment where you choose alignment over obligation, self-connection over self-criticism. 
Sleeping in, letting yourself take a break, choosing ease—those are not failures to do energy work, they are the work. The practice is not the ritual—it’s the quality of relationship you’re cultivating with yourself in how you BE with that choice.
People sometimes fall into thinking the purpose of energy work is to “earn” a manifestation. But the actual point is the process itself—how you feel while living your life, moment by moment. If the process doesn’t feel juicy, exciting, nourishing, interesting and good to your nervous system, then you’re missing the whole payoff. The universe isn’t rewarding discipline — it’s reflecting who you’re being in each moment. So how you feel in doing the energy work is what shifts you becoming able to see, choose, perceive, and intersect with things that resonate with your state. 
The energy you show up in to Everything you do, whether a formal practice to pre paving your energy for the day, or your moment to moment aware recalibration, it’s strengthening a set point that makes you available to cross paths with what matches your point of attraction. You become a suitor for synchronous rendezvous. 
 When someone says, “I didn’t do my morning energy practice because I slept in, or wanted to spend time with my kids,  it’s the choice to sleep in or spend the time with your kids that IS the energy work based on your relationship to that choice. That action was(or was not) your awareness of choice for alignment. If you listened to your body instead of overriding it with guilt or shame then that aware choice carries the frequency of self-honoring, and fuels you being resonant with more experiences that feel like relief, permission, and joy.
Energy work isn’t something you squeeze into your calendar. It’s the energy you bring to every choice. If you make it about chasing a perfect practice, you turn it into another job. If you make it about choosing what feels aligned— which could be a practice but also could be even tiny moments you bring awareness in, then you’re already doing energy work and are IN alignment the moment your awareness meets up with that choice. 
When you’re not sure if what choice you’re doing is truly birthed from alignment or just disguised obligation, you can pause and ask yourself:
Pure Desire Check
- Am I choosing this from fear of what happens if I don’t… or from love for what happens when I do?
- If my only job was to enjoy being alive today, what would I say yes to and call my energy work?
- Does this feel like love for me, or does it feel like proving me?
- If nothing needed fixing and I was already enough, what would feel delicious to do right now?
- If how this choice feels(while doing it), was the seed of my future, would I want more of it?
- If I stripped away the label of this activity being “healthy” or “right,” would my body still want it right now?
- Does this feel like love for me at this moment, or does it feel like punishment dressed as self-care/”doing the work”?
- If I never got credit for doing this — no results, no praise, no gold star — would I still want it?
- Does this choice feel like I’m adding aliveness or draining it?
- Am I practicing this to fix a lack, or to savor a state?
- If nothing ever changed outside me, would it still feel good to live in this feeling today?
✨ These questions are your Compass that make it impossible to hide behind “good for me” activities or to see if you are outsourcing your peace and joy to the “practice” versus “presence” with how that practice feels energetically.  It  shows whether the energy you’re carrying is aligned with love, permission, and aliveness — or guilt, proving, and performance.
The effectiveness of energy work isn’t in the ritual itself — it’s in the state you’re in while doing it. If the state is pressure, guilt, or exhaustion, then the “energy work” will carry that imprint. In that sense, yes, going to bed (even if it doesn’t completely restore you) is more aligned than doing the practice from a misaligned state.
Your nervous system is the foundation. If it’s fried, anything you add on top won’t land. Even imperfect regulation (sleep, rest, food, zoning out) may not fully restore, but it still supports your body’s attempt to downshift. That matters more than checking a box on doing an energetic practice.
If you skip energy work but hold yourself in guilt, you amplify dysregulation. But if you skip it with grace — “This is what my system needs right now” — then even sleep or avoiding becomes energy work. Alignment is less about the action and more about whether you’re in integrity with yourself as you choose it.
Sometimes “doing nothing” is actually the fastest way back to resonance. A nap, a meal, a cupcake or quiet rest might move you back into baseline more efficiently than struggling through a meditation or visualization that you’re not resourced for and trying to choke down alignment..
The real shift is when you stop layering guilt over what you did or didn’t do and see that just being in awareness around it, in and of itself is sacred nervous-system stewardship.

1. The Nervous System as the Real “Energy Work”
Yes—sometimes the very thing someone calls an excuse (being tired, hungry, lonely, busy, etc.) is actually the body’s wisdom saying, “This is what I need right now to come back into regulation.” If the nervous system is dysregulated, forcing yourself to do structured “energy work” on top of depletion can actually work against alignment. Meeting your body’s need first—through sleep, nourishment, rest, or connection—is energy work, because it restores the baseline state from which real alignment flows.
2. The Subtle Trap
However, there’s nuance: not everything that feels like relief in the moment, is truly regulation. For example, numbing on social media or procrastinating with tasks that leave you more drained might masquerade as “ease,” but they don’t restore. The difference is whether the choice actually brings ease and coherence to your system, or whether it’s a temporary escape that leaves you more scattered.
3. Reframing the “Excuse”
So whatever someone chooses might be your most aligned energy work in that moment—if it truly meets your nervous system’s need for safety, ease, or replenishment. The deeper discernment is: “Did that choice restore me to more coherence, or did it just avoid what felt uncomfortable?” Both are human, but only the first is truly aligned.
The Empowered Way to See It
Instead of beating yourself up for not doing “formal energy work,” you can ask:
- “Did my choice restore my nervous system?”
- “Do I feel more grounded after it?”
- “If not, what would actually feel regulating right now?” And just in answering those questions, you are back in alignment doing energy work.  
That shift allows all of life—sleep, eating, pausing, walking, rewriting, 2-2-2, calling a friend—to be seen as energy work when done consciously.
Even if someone doesn’t feel fully restored after choosing sleep, food, or rest, it can still be more aligned than pushing yourself to do “energy work” from a depleted, guilty, or resistant state because Alignment Is About State, Not Activity
- Integrity choices meet a real need (rest, nourishment, connection, quiet). They bring you closer to yourself. 
- Avoidance choices keep you away from yourself (scrolling to numb, busywork to avoid stillness, food to distract). 

1. Notice the Energy of the Choice
- Integrity feels clean: A quiet “yes, this is what I need right now.” There’s often a sense of relief, even if subtle. 
- Out of integrity feels noisy: Inner bargaining, over-explaining, guilt, or pressure. It’s more about avoiding discomfort than truly meeting yourself. 

2. Check the Aftertaste
Ask yourself: “When I imagine looking back on this choice tomorrow, will I feel that I honored myself, or that I hid from myself?”
- If tomorrow-you would thank you for honoring your body’s need, you’re in integrity. 
- If tomorrow-you would sigh and feel the same cycle of avoidance, you weren’t in integrity. 

3. Anchor in Self-Connection vs. Escape
- Integrity choices meet a real need (rest, nourishment, connection, quiet). They bring you closer to yourself. 
- Avoidance choices keep you away from yourself (scrolling to numb, busywork to avoid stillness, food to distract). 

4. The Body’s Signal
Your nervous system will often tell the truth faster than your mind.
- If the body softens (shoulders drop, breath deepens, tension releases), you’re aligned. 
- If the body still feels braced, buzzing, or unsettled, it may be an avoidance move. 

5. Ask the Integrity Question
A simple way to check in:
👉 “Am I choosing this to take care of myself, or to get away from myself?”


ANY PATH OFFERS HARMONY
1. Awareness
Integrity begins with awareness. Whatever you’re about to do — energy practice, sleep, eat, scroll, or rest — pause long enough to notice: “I am choosing this.”
2. Permission
From that awareness, grant yourself full permission. Say yes to the choice without layering guilt, shame, or mental gymnastics. Permission sounds like: “It’s okay for me to want this right now.”
3. Ownership
Once you’ve given permission, step into ownership. Claim the choice as yours — not something you “had” to do, not something to blame on exhaustion, busyness, or others. Ownership sounds like: “I chose rest tonight because my body needed it.”
4. The Aftertaste Check
Ask: “When I look back tomorrow, will I feel that I honored myself, or that I avoided myself?” Integrity is less about the action and more about whether it feels clean afterward.
5. Body Signals
Your body tells the truth faster than the mind. If shoulders drop, breath deepens, or tension eases, you’re in integrity. If you still feel braced or buzzing, it may be avoidance.
6. Honesty About Impact
Integrity doesn’t mean every choice restores you perfectly. It means you’re honest about the impact. You might say: “I didn’t feel fully restored after the nap, but it was still better than forcing energy work from guilt.” That honesty is part of the alignment.
7. The Integrity Question
Keep it simple:
👉 “Am I choosing this to take care of myself, or to get away from myself?”

✨ Summary:
To live in integrity is to choose with awareness, give yourself permission, claim ownership, and be honest about impact. From that place, even rest, food, or stepping away can be sacred energy work — because what matters most isn’t the activity, but the state of alignment you carry into and out of it.

✅ The Four Real Steps of Integrity
- Awareness — “I see this as a choice I’m making.” 
- Permission — “I allow myself to choose this without guilt.” 
- Ownership — “I claim this as my choice, not an excuse.” 
- Honesty About Impact — “I acknowledge what this choice gave me (or didn’t) without pretending.” 
That’s the backbone. Everything else (body signals, aftertaste check, the integrity question) are supportive tools people can use to help discern — but they’re not additional steps.
