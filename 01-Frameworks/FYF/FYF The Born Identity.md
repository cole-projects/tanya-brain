# FYF The Born Identity

The Born Identity

Sometimes people think they always need to be future casting to become their future self. But actually, if you’re already taking aligned action, you’re already integrating that identity.
Because in-spired action IS embodiment of your future you. 
If you’re already speaking up, making decisions, setting boundaries, or stepping into leadership — you’re not rehearsing anymore. You’re living it. And that means the identity is already landing in real time.

But sometimes you feel ready to expand…
You feel confident…
You believe you could step into that next version of yourself…
…but you haven’t taken much action yet.
Not because you don’t think it’s possible or that you can —
but because it’s still unfamiliar on what its like to move FROM that identity.
That’s where future casting becomes powerful.
Future casting lets you rehearse the emotional experience of being that version of you:
- What it feels like to lead
- What it feels like to speak confidently
- What it feels like to make bold decisions
- What it feels like to trust yourself
- What it feels like to BE successful 
And your mind doesnt know the difference between imagined doing experience vs actually doing it and so you can begin to emotionally visit that version of yourself.
And as you do, your nervous system starts to recognize it as safe… as normal… as…just you.
So when the moment for action arrives, it no longer feels like you’re stepping into something foreign.
It feels like you’re stepping into something familiar and natural 
And that’s when action becomes easier and doable from where you are at. 

Because you’re not forcing yourself to become someone new —
You’re simply just naturally stepping into a version of you that you’ve already started to become. Its pre-game practice so when you step onto the field you don’t freeze. Cuz when your body flinches, your brain begins its threat response.

So you may be thinking…but if rewriting truly works to clear resistance, then action should naturally follow easily.
So then why would I futurecast?
The key is:
Rewriting creates emotional permission, and it reveals to you what’s possible and opens up your belief in your potential. 
Future casting amplifies emotional familiarity with it and familiarity living FROM it.
And Action is the lived integration through external expression of it and begins the self perpetuating loop to ensure momentum. 

After a rewrite, someone might think:
“I actually believe I can do this.”
But they still might not:
- know what it feels like
- know how they carry themselves
- know how they’ll respond in the moment
- know what it’s like to live in that identity 
So rewriting removes:
“I can’t”
But future casting builds:
“This is what it’s like to be me when I can.”

For Example
If someone is rewriting resistance like
“I can’t lead this meeting”
They might shift that belief to
“But what if I could to I’m curious to know what that could be like to I actually could lead this meeting.”
But future casting helps them experience:
- Sitting confidently in the chair
- Speaking calmly
- Feeling steady when others disagree
- Staying grounded under pressure 
Now you’re not just believing it.
You’re experiencing it internally before it happens.
That reduces friction at game time so that your brain doesn’t derail you from the threat of unfamiliarity. 
Its like a practicum or internship in your mind before you actually step into action IRL fully.

So, 
Rewrite changes belief
Future casting changes felt familiarity living from it
Action changes identity which triggers the loop to ensure memory of that identity as salient and meaningful. What your brain notices and flags as important, relevant or significant. 
All three layers complete the arc. 

However…Sometimes rewriting alone is enough.
Sometimes:
- You do a rewrite 
- You feel aligned
- You naturally take action and live from embodiment 
But it is helpful to rehearse before the action to build equity with your brain's bank of safety and familiarity so that your rewriting fuel doesn’t only get you 10 minutes onto the stage or in the big game and all of a sudden you get stage fright or field fright. 

So When Is Future Casting Most Useful?
Future casting is most helpful when you:
- You’re expanding into a role or function that is something new and unfamiliar- external reality gap
- There is emotional distance between who you ARE now and who you are becoming. Internal reality gap
- Believe you can… but you’re still not moving
- You feel ready… but slightly hesitant
- Understand cognitively that it’s possible… but haven’t felt it emotionally

Not fear-based thoughts. 
Not blocked.
Just not yet embodied in experience.
Future casting fills that gap.
If blocked or fear, that would call for rewriting or nervous system safety. Buckets 1 and 2. 

Future casting is simply a bridge when embodiment needs strengthening

Future casting helps experience what it’s like to actually be the version of yourself who can — how you carry yourself, how you respond under pressure, how you speak, decide, and move. It turns possibility into a felt experience. 

Rewriting answers “Is this possible for me?”, future casting answers “What is it like when this is me?”, and action becomes the final integration where you are no longer imagining or rehearsing — you are simply living as that version of yourself.
Part 1 — Arrival & Opening
So today we’re not going to think about becoming a new version of yourself…
We’re going to step into one. Rehearsal time!
Because identity doesn’t change when you think differently.
Identity changes when your body experiences being someone different.
So Right now, I want you to sit comfortably…
Feet on the ground…
Shoulders relaxed…
Take a slow breath in…
And another…
Let your nervous system settle…
Arrive here. Be open to all of the facets of you. 

Part 2 — Choose a powerful Identity Source
Think of someone — real or fictional — who embodies the energy you want.
This could be:
- Someone you know and admire
- A public figure
- A fictional character from a movie
- A role model
- Even a “made up” powerful version of you

Someone who feels:
- Powerful
- Calm
- Confident
- Worthy
- Steady
- Bulletproof 
Examples:
- Calm CEO
- Loving grounded parent
- Confident speaker
- Someone who is deeply self-worthy
- Someone who is emotionally steady
- Someone who is decisive 
- An admired relationship

Just notice who comes to mind naturally.
Now imagine this person standing in front of you.
Just observe them.
You’re not becoming them…
You’re borrowing their energy.
You’re studying their energy.

Part 3 — Study the Energy
Close your eyes and imagine this person in front of you.
Then ask:
- How do they stand?
- How do they hold their shoulders?
- How do they breathe?
- How do they walk into a room?
- How do they respond when something is hard?
- How do they look at people?
- How do they talk to themselves?
- How do they stand?

If you had to play the role of them in a movie, how would you BE more like them?
What is the ENERGY of this person?

- Calm certainty
- Quiet confidence
- Grounded presence
- Soft power
- Self-trust
- Fully expressed
- Unapologetically happy
- Fearless and decisive
- Warm and inviting

Let yourself feel the tone of their presence.
You’re not copying behavior as you. 
You’re absorbing energy. Merging with theirs. 

Part 4 — Step Into Them (The Identity Shift)
Imagine stepping toward this person…
And stepping into their body
Like you’re putting on a suit…
You are now inside their body…
Seeing through their eyes…
Feeling through their body…
Let your posture shift slightly…
Sit like they would sit…
Breathe like they would breathe…
Hold your body like they would hold their body…
Feel what it’s like to be this person…
Feel their steadiness…
Feel their confidence…
Feel their calm…
Feel their worthiness…
Let that feeling spread through your chest…
Your shoulders…
Your stomach…
Your face…
Your breath…
You are now experiencing what it’s like to be them.

Part 5 — Identity Rehearsal
Now, staying in this identity… FROM this identity looking out, imagine a situation in your life happening now where you usually shrink or avoid or delay. 
Maybe:
- A conversation
- A decision
- A fear
- Something you’ve been avoiding
From this identity…from inside the suit of them,
How do you respond?”
- Someone challenges you — how do you respond?
- Something goes wrong — how do you react?
- You feel doubt — what do you say to yourself?
- You need to take action — what do you do?

Answer as that version.

This is identity rehearsal.
This is how your brain installs identity
Not thinking → but acting as
Notice how different it feels…
You’re not forcing confidence…
You’re being someone who already has it.

Part 6 — Physical Embodiment 
Now begin to physically embody this identity in your own body now
Sit a little differently…
Relax your shoulders…
Adjust your breathing…
Let your face soften…
Feel the steadiness in your body…
This is what it feels like to be someone who…
Is enough…
Is worthy…
Is powerful…
Is calm…
Is capable…
Let your nervous system memorize this feeling.

Part 7 — The Rehearsal Action
What is one small action this version of you would take today toward your goal that can be done within 15 minutes time?
See yourself taking that action…
Feel yourself doing it…
Experience the confidence…
The steadiness…
The self-trust…
This is you… rehearsing becoming.

Part 8 — Identity Anchor
Now choose one anchor:
A breath
A posture
A word
A gesture
Something that brings you back to this identity.
When you use this…
You return to this version of you.

Closing
Slowly come back to the room…
But notice something…
You didn’t imagine becoming someone else…
You accessed a version of you that already exists…
This is how identity shifts…
You rehearse the version…
Until it becomes natural…
Until it becomes you.
