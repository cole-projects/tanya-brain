# FYF Upgraded Problems

Community Call Activity: “Future You Has an Upgraded Problem”
This isn’t about pretending you feel confident or calm.
People with ‘better problems’ still feel stressed, annoyed, unsure.
The difference is the structure of their life — what’s already in motion. Their life already has momentum. Things are happening because they're participating, not waiting.
So because momentum comes from participation, not confidence, we're going to use the prove Loop to step into the kind of life that already has things in motion. 

STEP 1 — Name the Upgraded Problem
Think of something you feel stuck in right now.
Now imagine a version of you one year from now where things are working.
That version still has problems — just different ones.
What problem would that version of you be dealing with instead?
- Don’t overthink it
- Choose something annoying but solvable
- Write the first real answer 
Example Answers
- “Choosing which opportunity to focus on”
- “Protecting my time and energy”
- “Deciding what to say no to”
- “Managing demand or expectations”
- “Balancing growth with rest”
- “Maintaining momentum instead of starting over”
- “Delegating instead of doing everything myself” 

STEP 2 — Structural Truths
What has to already be true about someone’s life for this to be the problem?
Have them write 3 truths.
- These are conditions, not emotions 
- If this is the problem, something is already working
Example Answers
(For: Choosing between opportunities)
- “Opportunities are showing up”
- “People see value in me”
- “I’m visible and engaged”
- “I’m no longer in pure survival mode”
- “I’m participating, not waiting”
- “Things are moving, even if imperfect”
(For: Protecting my time)
- “People want access to me”
- “My time matters”
- “I have responsibilities I didn’t before”
- “I’m no longer trying to prove myself”
- “My energy is a limited resource”

STEP 3 — Orientation Shift
Given that these things are already true…how does someone whose life looks like this move differently through today?
Then ask them to finish one of these sentences:
- “Someone like this stops ______.” 
- “Someone like this starts ______.” 
- “Someone like this decides ______.” 
Example Answers
- “I stop overexplaining”
- “I stop waiting for permission”
- “I start responding instead of reacting”
- “I decide faster”
- “I don’t chase clarity — I create it”
- “I move even when it’s messy”
- “I trust myself sooner.”

STEP 4 — The 24-Hour Match
What’s one small thing Future You would do today —not big, not dramatic — just different?
What is one concrete action that expresses that orientation today?
Rules:
- Under 10 minutes
- Doesn’t depend on anyone else
- Slightly grown-up, not intense 
Example Actions
- Send the message without rewriting it
- Take the walk instead of spiraling
- Close the loop on something unfinished
- Say no to one thing on the calendar.
- Stop checking your phone after sending the message.
- Choose one option instead of waiting for perfect clarity.
- End a conversation earlier.
Have them circle one.
STEP 5 — Lock the Meaning (Identity Integration)
This step answers the real question the nervous system is asking after action:
What does this say about me?”
After you’ve chosen and done (or committed to) your Step 4 action, we’re going to lock the meaning of that action — because identity is shaped by what actions mean, not just what you do.
- This action counts as evidence that I am becoming someone who ______.
- This is evidence that I am the kind of person who ______.
- Actions like this are how someone who ______ operates.
- This is what it looks like when I live from ______ instead of waiting.
- This is me practicing being someone who ______.
- This choice reflects a version of me who ______.
