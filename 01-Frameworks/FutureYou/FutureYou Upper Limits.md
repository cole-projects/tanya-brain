# FutureYou Upper Limits

🌟 FUTUREYOU Frequency Upper Limits
🔹 Opening Prompt Today we’re exploring what happens when things start going better. When you start feeling stabilized, when you get the job, the client, the clarity, the opportunity… and then somehow we self-sabotage, freeze, distract, or doubt.
This is just your nervous system maxing out of its current identity capacity for ‘good’ in your life. This is a dip. Soul stretch, next level opening. It’s not a bad thing…ever. 
This moment can be you standing at the edge going from surviving to optimizing. Or from optimizing at one level and growing into your next level of expansion. It does not need to be going from bad to good. It also occurs when you go from good to better. And better to amazing. Its the dance between whatever your lower limit patterns are that keep you safe in the level you are in and moving to your upper limits that are ready to be shattered by a FutureYou who’s already living into your next level expansion. 
🧠 Common Thoughts When You Hit a Limit
- “I can’t handle this.”
- “I’m not ready.”
- “This is too good to be true.”
- “What if this disappears?”
- “I’ll screw it up eventually.”
- “I don’t want to outshine others.”
- “Who am I to have it this good?”
- “Now that I got this, something bad will probably happen.” 
😨 Common Feelings
- Sudden anxiety after success
- Random arguments or drama out of nowhere
- Emotional numbing or overthinking
- Exhaustion or procrastination
- Panic about time or responsibility
- Guilt or shame when things go well

🔹 Micro Teaching 
Dr. Benjamin Hardy and Gay Hendricks both teach about upper limits as invisible thresholds where success, love, abundance, or peace feel too unfamiliar to be safe. Meanwhile, lower limits are the baseline survival patterns you fall back into when you feel unsafe—overworking, people-pleasing, checking out, self-doubt, perfectionism. That’s you surviving the lower limit identity. Most people live ping-ponging between upper and lower limits, never stabilizing in expansion. What’s the secret sauce? Your Purpose Pull. It is the energy signature of your FutureYou—it doesn’t push from fear, it pulls from your next CHAPTER(all parts of same story, all facets of the whole self. Knowing this helps you integrate the old versions with the new version you are becoming so you move forward without resistance and without disconnection from your SELVES which is disconnection from Source. YOU source your happiness and it is sourced to the degree your Source is connected and whole- is one. When you are one, you are one, you are ALL of you. And when you are all of you, you are connected with the all that is. 
Expansion doesn’t require force. It requires safety in identity. And your identity is made up of your self image and to that which you are most committed to. Your beliefs.  You don’t rise because you force yourself upward—you rise because you normalize a new frequency. A new belief about you that feels safe in your body and in your brain.  You don’t break your upper limit by doing more—you break it by BEING more - more regulated in your next level self.
The upper limit(dip) is not a wall. It’s just a frequency threshold that your nervous system hasn’t been taught to call home… yet. We can experience this upper limit minute to minute or can go long stretches and then hit it. But make no mistake, it’s not a problem or something going wrong. It’s an indicator. It’s your internal guidance system signaling you and passing you clues in the only language it knows and it SPEAKS to you THROUGH you. Through the vessel in which YOU, the real you, is housed. It’s a sacred language being spoken between your higher self(Source/true self/energetic expansive you) and your avatar(physical self/self image) about your readiness for your next level. 
Let’s talk about Survival
If you’ve been in survival for a long time, an upper limit is the moment when survival is no longer the driver—but you haven’t yet anchored into a purpose that feels powerful enough to justify ease, fun, or expansion. It can feel like a purpose void. And ironically, it’s exactly where many people hit their next upper limit.
When moving beyond survival, your purpose/why needs to evolve. 
In survival, your purpose is obvious: Make it past a problem. Get through. Fix the pain. Prove you’re safe.
It’s raw, urgent, and focused.
But once you start to stabilize—once the bills are paid, the nervous system is regulated, or the big crisis has passed—the original fuel behind your action disappears.
And that’s where people crash into an upper limit.
Not because you can’t go further…but because you haven’t yet connected to a purpose that’s expansive enough to sustain ease. When you’re no longer surviving, your “why” has to upgrade from survival to expansion.And that can feel disorienting at first.
You might think:
- “What’s the point now?”
- “Is this selfish?”
- “Shouldn’t I be helping others instead?”
- “Is it okay to just want fun?” 
But here’s the truth:
🌀 The moment you no longer need your purpose to SAVE you… is the moment it can start to expand you. This is the journey of moving from lack energy to abundance energy. It’s the key. You can’t have a happy ending. 
Abraham Hicks teaches that we came here for the joy of the expansion itself.  Not to fix what’s broken—but to play in what’s becoming.
So if you feel lost after surviving, or like your why evaporated…
You’re not broken. You’re upgrading. Let your purpose evolve into something that doesn’t demand you struggle.
Let it be about joy. Beauty. Impact. Curiosity. Freedom.These are all of the FEELINGS we are accessing when we do a futurecast. We use details ONLY as an access point or bridge to a feeling so that we are resonant with the frequency of the THING that we want because we only want the thing because of what we believe it will make us feel. The things don’t us feelings.  That’s backwards. It’s our feelings that bring us all the things. 
What if your next level doesn’t require you to prove anything, fight anything, or survive anything?
What if the only thing your purpose asks now… is to feel more like yourself every day?
What if thriving is the point—and everything else flows from that?

Abraham Hicks perspective:
- “You don’t hit an upper limit because you’re not ready—you hit it because your vibration hasn’t yet normalized the new frequency.”
-  Your set point of vibration is what determines what you allow. When you start receiving more than what you’ve been habitually feeling and believing, you pinch yourself off—not because it’s not yours, but because you haven’t yet emotionally practiced the frequency of “this is normal for me.”
- “It’s never about effort, it’s always about allowing.”
- The upper limit is just a vibrational mismatch between the new, expanded reality and your practiced thoughts. It’s not a ceiling; it’s a moment to tune up your focus and deliberately lean into the feeling of already having become it.
- “Your inner being never limits you. Only your practiced thoughts do.”
- That contraction you feel when you’re growing? It’s not fear of success—it’s simply that you’re thinking a thought your inner being doesn’t agree with, like “I can’t handle this,” “This is too good to be true,” or “I always mess it up.” That dissonance is your guidance system showing you what’s ready to be upgraded.
- When your nervous system flinches, it’s just your practiced vibration catching up. It’s not wrong. It’s just asking: can I let this be safe? Can I let this be normal now?”
🌀 Bashar perspective:
- “Your upper limit is not a limit—it’s a reflection of the definition you still hold about yourself.”
- Any recurring resistance is showing you a belief you’ve unconsciously accepted as true. When you redefine yourself consciously and act in alignment with your excitement (without insistence on outcome), the so-called “limit” dissolves.
- “When you get close to your purpose frequency, survival mechanisms get louder—not because you’re wrong, but because you’re on the edge of a reality shift.”
- Fear is a sign of a belief that is out of alignment with your excitement. The discomfort is actually the doorway—if you go through it, you recalibrate your timeline.REPEAT.
- “Excitement is the compass. Survival patterns only activate when you ignore the compass or misunderstand fear as truth.” REPEAT!
- Bashar distinguishes between fear that’s a warning (rare) and fear that’s just the breaking apart of old definitions. When you hit your “limit,” he’d say to thank the fear, ask “what belief must I hold to feel this?” and replace it with a higher-definition version of yourself.
🌀 Joe Dispenza perspective
- “If you can emotionally condition your body into the feeling of the future before it’s happened, you’re no longer waiting for the external world to tell you who you are—you’re telling your body ahead of time.”
- You’re not waiting for the world to prove who you are—you’re practicing the frequency so your body and reality get the memo at the same time.
- He says: start with inner proof.
That can be:
- A physical action (walking tall like FutureYou)
- A mental rehearsal (imagining that sales call or client convo as already done)
- A felt emotion (gratitude before the thing happens)
- “You’re giving your body a taste of the future until it believes it’s the past.”
🌀 Thomas Hübul perspective
(Relational and collective trauma work through lresence, integration, and slowing down the healing impulse)
He brings a trauma-informed, somatic, and relational layer to this work —especially for people who freeze not because they’re resisting growth, but because they are touching an old wound or frozen time capsule in the body.
His nuanced addition would be:
- “When you freeze at the edge of growth, it may not be resistance—it may be an unmetabolized moment resurfacing for integration.”
- Slowing down is a sacred pause to digest what couldn’t be felt before.
- Some upper limits aren’t energetic ceilings—they’re relational or developmental ruptures that require witnessing, not pushing through it. Empathy as an antidote. Brene
- The task isn’t to override the freeze with affirmations or action—it’s to build capacity to be with what’s arising.  Something old may be surfacing to be held in new safety.”
- “What part of you is waiting to be seen, not shifted?”
- “You’re not stuck—you’re in a moment of inner reunion.”
🛠 What to Do (From Hübl’s Lens):
- Slow the process down to meet the body’s pace.
- Bring in presence
- Allow integration of fragmented parts that couldn’t previously come online
- Feel without fixing—sometimes your only job is to be a “loving witness” to what’s arising
- Ask yourself.. is there a frozen part of me that needs warmth before it can move?
This is especially crucial when someone:
- Feels chronically stuck or overwhelmed
- Can’t make a move no matter how many mindset tools you try
- Feels like you’re regressing during expansion
- Is deeply sensitive or carrying ancestral/family field weight

🔹 Future Casting Meditation (7–9 mins)
“Your Purpose-Pulled Life Beyond the Ceiling”
- Before the meditation:   “As Bashar teaches, if fear or contraction arises, it’s often just the shell of your old identity cracking open. It means you’re near the threshold. Breathe it in, and walk through the portal.” 
🌟 You don’t wait to be the version of you who has it. You show your system what that feels like now, in whatever way you can most naturally access—and that’s what reshapes your timeline.”
You’re giving yourself proof when you do the small thing and you’re telling your body ahead of time that you already are that when you acknowledge it within yourself.”

Begin script:
Close your eyes and bring your attention inward.

Inhale… and exhale…

Let your body settle into this now moment. Let your system know you are safe here and you are safe to dream here.

Imagine a version of you who has shattered the limits that you are brushing up against. You can feel the veil but can almost reach to the other side. Imagine just beyond is the version of you who completely dissolved the old ceilings.

The version of you who no longer flinches when things go well.

Who no longer contracts when joy, wealth, or support flows in.

Feel this version of you standing in a moment of your everyday life. Not a dream life. A normalized life. A stable, magnetic life. Get a few seconds glimpse of them. 

See the way your FutureYou moves…
Hear the tone of their voice…
Feel the absence of urgency in their body…

This version of you knows their worth.
This version doesn’t strive to “become”—they remember.

Now picture that one tiny specific moment from their day:
- Where are they?
- What are they doing that used to feel too big, too much, too far?
- How do you imagine it feels now in their body?
Let your cells soak it in. Let your heart stabilize here.

You are not preparing to become.
You are visiting your frequency home.

And every breath is a signal to the universe:

“I can handle more.
I am safe in joy.
I’m ready to rise.”

Breathe in your FutureYou.

When you’re ready, open your eyes slowly.

🔹 Journaling Prompts 
🧭 Journal Prompts if coming out of survival:
🔹 “If I no longer needed to survive, strive, or prove—what would my purpose want to become?”
🔹 “What feels deliciously worth it now that I’m no longer in survival?”
🔹 “What would expansion for expansion’s sake look like in my life?”
Gentle entry points when the why isn’t clear yet
🌱 1. Let your body speak first.
🔹 “What part of you relaxes when you imagine not having to fix anything anymore?”
🔹 “Where in your body feels the most peaceful when you imagine doing something just because it feels like you?”
✨ Why this helps: Your body often recognizes safety and alignment before the mind can name a new purpose. Let sensation lead.

🌤 2. Look for micro-moments of meaning (not big-picture purpose).
🔹 “Tell me about a moment lately that felt oddly light or satisfying—even if it seemed small or unrelated.”
🔹 “When do you feel most like yourself—even if no one sees it?”
✨ Why it helps: Instead of asking yourself to name a north star, name a spark. These are breadcrumbs that lead to the deeper why.

🌀 3. Use contrast to find your hidden desires.
🔹 “If you could delete one pattern or pressure from your life forever, what would be left that feels like freedom?”
🔹 “What are you tired of having to be the reason for your motivation?”
✨ Why it helps: The push-away points often reveal the pull-toward desires underneath. Contrast provides clarity. 

🧭 4. Anchor in the idea that purpose can be felt before it is named.
🔹What part of you is waiting to be seen, not shifted?”

You don’t need to name your new purpose yet.
You just need to recognize what you’re being pulled toward.
The part of you that’s not trying to prove anything.
The part that breathes easier.
That’s your new purpose speaking… not with words, but with resonance.
Right now, your only job is to notice what feels like more of you.
That’s the bridge.
You don’t have to force a mission.
You just have to stop mistaking struggle for worthiness.

🧭 Journal Prompts if moving from one optimized level to the next:
Answer with bold honesty and emotional detail:
- “I know I’m hitting an upper limit when…”   (Describe the patterns—procrastination, fear, withdrawal, overdoing. What’s your tell?) 
- “If I stopped making decisions from survival, I would…”   (What would change immediately? What would no longer make sense to tolerate?) 
- Describe a snapshot moment of your FutureYou living beyond your upper limit—what are they doing, feeling, receiving, embodying?   (Include sensory details: body posture, environment, inner calm, outer evidence.) 
- “The safest way I can support my expansion right now is…”   (What does safety in growth look like for you? What stabilizing action, thought, or environment could become your new baseline?)
- Optional journal prompt:   “What belief would I have to hold in order to see this expansion as dangerous or overwhelming?”   (Then ask: Do I still believe that? Or is this just an old echo I’m ready to outgrow?) 

🔹 Optional Integration Practice / Homework
For the next 7 days, every time something good happens, pause.
Say to yourself out loud or in your journal:

“This is my new normal. I can handle this. I am safe here.”

Track what emotions come up—celebration, discomfort, guilt, resistance.
You’re not wrong for feeling them whether it’s celebration or resistance. You’re just meeting your edge… and expanding it.
