# FutureYou Past Cast

(Pre-Frame to Past-Cast Work)
Take a moment to settle where you are.
Let your body arrive in the chair.
No effort—just notice contact, support, gravity.
Now choose a neutral or pleasant memory from your past.
Nothing intense.
Something simple and safe.
You don’t need to relive it yet.
Just acknowledge that the memory exists.
Allow that memory to appear out in front of you,
as if you’re watching a scene from a comfortable distance.
You are not inside the scene.
You are watching yourself in it.
Take a moment to orient.
Notice:
- Where the image is located in space 
- How far away it feels 
- Whether you see your whole body or just part of yourself 
Stay here.
Let the context of the scene begin to organize itself.
Without stepping in, notice:
- The environment around you 
- The general layout of the space 
- What tells you where you are 
Let the scene become clearer from a distance.
Now allow yourself to notice the visual details, still observing.
The quality of light.
The shapes.
The depth of the scene.
You’re not trying to feel anything yet—
just letting the picture stabilize.
Now notice the sound, if there is any.
You don’t need to turn it up.
Just notice whether it’s present or faint.
Near or far.
Stay observing.
From this observer position, check in with your body.
Notice how involved your body feels right now.
Stay outside the scene.
Now, while keeping the image exactly where it is,
notice there is a version of you inside the scene.
That version of you has:
- A posture 
- A breathing rhythm 
- A way of standing or sitting 
You’re still watching.
Take a moment to notice that body
before stepping into it.
And when you’re ready…
Step into the scene.
Let your perspective shift smoothly
so you’re seeing through your own eyes.
Allow the image to become life-size.
Notice how close everything feels now.
Give your body a moment to adjust.
Notice:
- Where your feet are 
- How your weight is distributed 
- How your breath is moving 
Stay here long enough for your body to settle.
Now gently bring your attention to the sounds.
Notice one sound that’s already there.
Let it come a little closer.
A little clearer.
Turn the volume up gradually—
only as much as feels natural.
Pause.
Notice how your body responds.
Now notice the color of the scene.
Ask yourself:
Is this scene more color or black-and-white?
Whatever it is, begin to shift it slowly.
If it’s black-and-white,
allow color to come in gently.
Not all at once—layer by layer.
If it’s already in color,
soften it slightly…
then bring the color back richer and clearer.
Notice what changes internally as the color shifts.
Now choose one specific sensory detail.
It might be:
- A particular sound 
- A visual detail 
- A physical sensation in your body 
Bring your attention fully to that one element.
Let it sharpen.
Let it become more present.
Stay associated.

Formal NLP Anchor Installation
As you remain fully inside this experience,
notice where the feeling is strongest in your body.
When it reaches a natural peak…
👉 Press your thumb and finger together now.
Hold the pressure steady.
Stay inside the experience.
Let your nervous system register the state.
Take one slow breath.
And release your fingers.
Good.

Now gently step back out of the scene.
Return to observing it from a distance.
Notice how the feeling shifts.
Now once again…
Step back into the scene.
As you do,
press your thumb and finger together again.
Notice how quickly the state returns.
Release.
And step back out.
Take a moment to return your attention to the room.

Pre-Frame Transition Into Past-Cast
Notice something important.
When you were watching the memory,
you understood it.
When you were inside the memory,
your body recognized it.
In the next process, we’ll work entirely from the inside position—
not to analyze the past,
but to access the state your body already knows how to generate.
Stay with that awareness as we begin.


—————
Past casting is is a powerful move because you’re re-activating a known, embodied frequency the nervous system already trusts, then letting that state bleed into the now.
I want you to recall a past moment that has the qualities of what your futurecast contains.  
Rather than narrating or, I want you to stay inside the moment. 
Narrating the story means mental explanation— talking about the memory — explaining, analyzing, or giving context from the mind.
Staying inside the moment is an embodied experience which means experiencing the memory as if it’s happening now — through body sensations, breath, posture, and felt sense.
“Don’t tell me what happened. Let me feel where you are.”
1. Locate the Exact Moment (Anchor the Scene)
Goal: Eliminate story. Enter a single frame.
- What would you feel if your manifestation was actualized?
- “Take me to a specific moment in your past when you felt that same feeling or borrow it if you can’t access it [confident / safe / free / aligned / capable].” 
- “Where were you, exactly?” 
- “What time of day was it?” 
- “What had just happened right before this moment?”
- “Go to one specific moment in your past where your body knew things were okay.”
- “Freeze the scene at its peak. Don’t explain it—just be there.”
(If they narrate: “Pause the story. Stay inside the moment.”)

2. Re-Enter Through the Senses (Somatic Recall)
Goal: Reactivate the nervous system state, not the memory. This is where the energy comes back online.
- “What do you see around you right now in that moment?”
- “What sounds are present?”
- “What’s the temperature on your skin?”
- “What are you wearing? How does it feel on your body?”
- “What’s your posture like without changing it?”
- What’s the quality of stillness or movement in your body?”
- What does your body feel like from the inside right now?”
(If they go cognitive, gently redirect: “Stay with the body, not the meaning.“Let your body answer before your mind does.”)

3. Activate the Internal State (Identity & Nervous System)
Goal: Restore self-concept without affirmations.Bring the way of being forward.These questions bypass worthiness and go straight to certainty.
- “What do you know about yourself in this moment?”
- “What feels settled or unquestioned here?”
- “What aren’t you trying to prove?”
- “What feels unnecessary to worry about?”
- “In this moment, who are you not worried about being?”
- “What do you trust without needing proof?”

4. Pull the Energy Forward (Bridge to Now)
Goal: Identify the transferable energy. This is the integration point.
- If this moment had a tone or frequency, what would it be?”
- “If that version of you could step into your body right now, what would shift first?”
- “What part of that energy is still available to you today?”
- “Where in your body do you feel this state right now?”
- “If you let this feeling turn the volume up by 10%, what changes?”

5. Present-Moment Installation (Embodiment Cue)
Goal: Goal: Collapse past → present. Lock the state in physically, somatically.
- “Let your breath match this version of you naturally. 
- “Let your posture organize itself around this feeling.”
- “What’s one small movement this energy naturally wants to make?” “Let this version of you step into your body now.”
- “What changes immediately when they arrive?”
- “Where do you feel this energy landing in your body right now?”
“Let your posture organize itself around this feeling.”

6. Identity Reinforcement (Subtle, Non-Affirmational)
Goal: Create a lasting imprint. Keep it believable and non-triggering.
- “This isn’t something you created—it’s something you remembered. How does that land?”
- “What does this prove about what’s already inside you?”
- “What does your body now know that your mind forgot?”
- “What does this moment remind you is still true about you?”
- “What did your body just remember that it forgot?”
- “What becomes unnecessary to struggle with from this state?” 
7. DIRECTIONAL BRIDGE (Past → Future)
Goal: Aim the same embodied state toward the future without visualization or effort.
- “From here, what feels like it wants to come next?”
- “If this version of you were moving toward what you want, what would feel obvious or unnecessary?”
- “What does your body assume will work out from this state?”
- “What is the smallest next step that feels natural from here?”
