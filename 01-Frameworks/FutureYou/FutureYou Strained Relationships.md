🔹 Theme of the Week: Strained Relationships (3–5 mins)
Whether it’s family, partners, friends, or even coworkers—strained relationships carry weight in our field.
When connection feels fractured, heavy, or tense, it’s easy to believe that clarity or peace can only come from THEM changing.
But in energy work, we don’t wait on the external. We don't want our peace and happiness to be conditional upon people and circumstances outside of you.  So we meet what’s showing up with curiosity and self-responsibility and self love. 
Relationships don’t just live in our memories or text threads—they live in our energy field.
And that field is malleable.
We can shift our relationship experience by tuning into the frequency that we’re broadcasting—whether that's a frequency of resentment, blame, neediness, fear… or compassion, sovereignty, clarity, and love. We are always flowing energy whether we realize it or not, and you have a choice on the quality of that flow.
We’re not bypassing real hurt.
We’re acknowledging it, then choosing to interact with it from the version of us who is no longer at war within.
So why does this even matter?
It matters because the quality of your relationships shapes your nervous system.
And your nervous system shapes your reality.Meaning the state of your nervous system, whether it's regulated or dysregulated directly influences how you interpret, respond to, and create your reality. Your nervous system filters everything. It filters what you notice versus what you'll overlook. it filters what feels safe versus what feels threatening. it filters what you interpret as love versus danger, and what opportunities you allow yourself to receive and whether you can stay present or default to defensive patterns. If your nervous system is in a state of chronic stress or shut down or hypervigilance you're not seeing reality or the whole of it that's available to you but you're seeing it through the lens of survival. That lens distorts truth, limits connection, and filters potential  through the question of is this safe rather than is this true or aligned. but when your system is regulated your body signals safety, your awareness expands, and you interact with life from clarity instead of coping. That's what shapes your reality, your energy, your choices, your interpretations, they all get filtered through the state of your nervous system. Quote time! we see the world not as it is but as we are. 
This is why caring about how you feel(rather than whats true about the others persons intentions or words) is your number one job. Because when you process pain through the eyes of your wounded self, you reinforce that story that you're still in danger, rejected, unworthy or powerless. But when you interact with that pain from your healed self, yourself who is no longer at war within, you change everything. You become the part of you that no longer needs to blame or beg, and that doesn't shrink to stay connected. that can feel pain without becoming pain and know how to love yourself without conditions. This healed version doesn't bypass the pain but holds it with compassion like saying to yourself yes that hurt deeply, but I trust who I am now. I'm not at war with myself over what happened. I don't need to turn myself into someone else to be safe or loved anymore. And from that stance you release needing the other person to change, to apologize or to get you or see you. you reclaim your power. you metabolize the pain without carrying it forward. that's the version Of you who can love again. trust again. decide again, without flinching. 
Forgive, not because the person deserves forgiveness but because YOU deserve peace 
Strained dynamics can pull us into loops:
- needing closure to feel free 
- waiting for the other to “get it” 
- replaying what we should have said 
- fearing the next interaction 
- blaming ourselves 
But the future version of you doesn’t stay in that loop.
They don’t need everyone to agree with them to feel safe.
They’ve learned to discern what’s theirs, what isn’t, and how to hold their boundaries AND compassion at the same time.
So today, we’re not fixing the relationship.
We’re healing the part of YOU still holding it in your field with tension, attachment, or fear.
This is the reclamation of energetic sovereignty—and it’s magnetic.
Who feels this? Pop an “R” in the chat.if you are READY to RECLAIM that inner power. 
Let’s meet the version of you who already feels free.

🔸 1. “The Mirror Hold”
How to Do It:
- Place both hands on your cheeks or the sides of your jaw 
- Elbows tucked inward like a soft frame around your face 
- Eyes can be closed or gazing downward 
- Sit still and breathe, feeling your own presence being held 
This helps your own self-witnessing and self-holding. This is powerful because it mimics the loving touch of a parent or trusted presence—except it’s you holding you, as if to say to yourself, 
 “I see you. I hear you. I’m not leaving.”


💓 PHASE 2: The Retrieval – “Come Back to You”
Chair-Friendly Movement:

🌞 We are going to close with a song called “Long Time Sun” – Traditional Kundalini Closing
This song is traditionally sung at the end of a Kundalini Yoga class or meditation practice to seal the energy, send blessings, and invoke peace. 
✨ The 3-Part Blessing Practice 
1. First time – For Yourself
May the long time sun shine upon ME,
All love surround me,
And the pure light within me
Guide my way on.
2. Second time – For Your Community / the World
May the long time sun shine upon us,
All love surround us,
And the pure light within us
Guide our way on.
3. Third time – For Someone You’re Sending Love To
(You silently or gently say their name before you begin)
May the long time sun shine upon [them],
All love surround [them],
And the pure light within [them]
Guide their way on.

The layered repetition (Me → Us → You) reflects the Law of Oneness: we’re all connected, and what we wish for others flows back to us. So energetically, this blesses the self, the collective, and someone specific in your heart field — extending your vibration outward in ripples.

Let your hands rest gently over your heart or in your lap.
Begin to notice your breath…
Slow it down…
Let each inhale gather your scattered energy…
And each exhale melt the tension you’ve been holding.
Let the music hold you—like a river beneath everything you’re feeling.

Now… I want you to bring one person to mind.
Someone with whom your relationship feels strained, complicated, or unresolved.
Just let their image appear gently.
No pressure to fix. No pressure to figure anything out.
Just witness what shows up.
Now imagine them standing in front of you.
Not to argue. Not to explain.
Just to be seen.
Notice how your body responds.
Notice what emotions rise.
You are safe here.
This moment is not about them.
It’s about your energy…
Your healing…
Your freedom.

Now, from this space of grounded presence, say quietly to yourself:
“I see the pattern. I see the pain. But I choose peace.”
You’re not erasing what happened.
You’re ending the contract that says you have to carry it all.
Imagine a cord—an energetic thread—running between you and them.
This thread may be tangled in blame, guilt, need, or longing.
You don’t need to rip it.
Just loosen it…
Breathe gently as it begins to dissolve.
“I send back what’s not mine. And I call back all of me.”

“I release them from my field, with grace.”

You… standing tall.
Whole.
Clear.
Un-entangled.

Now bring your hand back to your heart and say inwardly:
“I don’t need closure to feel complete.”
“I don’t need their permission to be free.”
“I am no longer waiting—I am choosing.”
“I am safe to love without losing myself.”
“I am safe to walk forward. Clean. Clear. Sovereign.”
Let the music rise and carry you.
Stand in the field of your own becoming.
No longer defined by the pain…
No longer wired for war…
But grounded in grace.
Rooted in truth.
And wide open to the next chapter.
