# Ubiquitous Assimilation

## From the Film Detachment (2011)

In the film Detachment (2011), the phrase "ubiquitous assimilation. Doublethink." is used in a classroom speech and can be briefly explained as follows:

- **Ubiquitous assimilation** refers to the idea that societal norms, advertising, media messages and consumer culture are constantly everywhere ("ubiquitous"), and that people are pressured to absorb, imitate or conform to these messages ("assimilation").

- **Doublethink** (a term borrowed from Nineteen Eighty-Four by George Orwell) refers to the ability or compulsion to hold two contradictory beliefs simultaneously and believe both.

- In the film's context, the character uses them together to critique how people internalise messages such as "you must be thin to be happy" or "consumption equals success," even though the same culture warns them not to care about appearance or not to buy into the system. The speech suggests we're being told contradictory things (doublethink) and we are absorbing them constantly (ubiquitous assimilation).

**In short:** the line is a comment on the way modern society pushes people to conform to pervasive messages while simultaneously expecting them to believe in contradictory ideals — creating a mental conflict and loss of true individual thinking.
