# Power Reclamation (Frank's Question)

## You Are the Source — Not the Tool

What I'm hearing isn't "I don't know if cold calling works." It's more like: "I'm assuming there's a universal way this works or doesn't work, and I'm trying to decide if it's worth the discomfort before I move."

That belief alone puts you in a hesitant, guarded state — and that state is actually what determines the outcome, not the activity itself.

**Nothing "works" or "doesn't work" on its own.** Things work based on the energy and identity you bring to them — how you show up, what you expect, how open or constricted you are while doing it. That state shapes how you speak, how people feel you, what opportunities you notice, and what unfolds next.

Right now, the discomfort isn't a signal to stop doing it — it's just showing you that you're trying to take action while outsourcing the result. You're asking the action to prove something before you trust yourself.

---

## The Purpose of Action

The purpose of action isn't "make cold calls → get sales." That is limiting.

**The purpose of action is to increase the momentum of energy.** If the current state of energy is doubt, action is going to increase doubt. The rewrite and change of perspective is remembering that you are the source that decides how a tool or practice is used — so you can move from waiting to see if it works into knowing you're the one who makes things work.

Sometimes the payoff is a sale. Sometimes it's clarity, confidence, momentum, a connection, or a door you didn't expect — but it all matches the state you're operating from, not the specific tactic.

So the question isn't "Should I rewrite this so I can force myself to do it?" It's: **"What state do I want to generate from — and can I take action from that, without demanding a specific outcome?"**

The target is just a north star. The real work is you learning to trust yourself as the source.

---

## The Knife Analogy

Cold calling isn't a magic formula any more than a knife is. A knife can hurt, or it can cut a juicy steak — the power isn't in the tool, it's in the hands and intention using it.

The same is true here. No activity is inherently "worth it" or "not worth it." It becomes effective based on the state, clarity, and self-trust of the person using it.

When someone believes they are the source, they use tools differently — with presence, curiosity, and steadiness — and that's what creates results. Often those results come in ways that are bigger, more fluid, or more fulfilling than originally expected… even if they don't look exactly like the outcome they were aiming at.

---

## The Hidden Desire

"More sales" isn't the problem. The problem is using the desire to demand certainty and safety before acting. The hidden desire underneath is something like:

- I want proof or certainty this will work so I don't waste time
- I want reassurance before I expose myself to discomfort
- I want to know the stability of the outcome before I trust myself

That's not a wrong desire — it's just a safety-based desire, not an expansion-based one. And when action is driven by safety-seeking, it naturally stalls because it can't grow or become more — you cut yourself off from being the conduit that moves the energy further.

The desire doesn't need to be rewritten. **The relationship to the desire does.**

Instead of: "I'm cold calling to get sales."

The energetic upgrade is: **"I'm cold calling to practice being the source of momentum, connection, and opportunity — sales are one of the ways that can show up."**

Same action. Different state.

---

## The Power Reclamation Formula

### 1. Change What Counts as "Proof"

If proof equals sales only, your nervous system stays tight and guarded.

Redefine proof as:
- I showed up clean and present
- I stayed regulated during discomfort
- I spoke clearly and confidently
- I created a real human moment

Sales are a byproduct of this state — not the prerequisite.

---

### 2. Call from Contribution, Not Extraction

Two people can say the same words. One sounds desperate, one sounds grounded.

Before each call, orient to: "I'm here to see if I can help or clarify something, not to get something from them."

That single shift:
- Softens tone
- Improves listening
- Increases trust
- Keeps energy open even if they say no

People feel this instantly.

---

### 3. Narrow the Time Horizon

Don't "do cold calling." Do:
- 10 minutes
- 5 calls
- One clean round

Short intervals keep resistance low and allow momentum to regenerate — exactly like micro-stepping.

---

### 4. Use the Call to Practice Being the Source

After each call, ask:
- Did I stay connected to myself?
- Did I recover quickly from rejection or surprise?
- Did I remain open instead of collapsing?

That recovery is proof. That's the muscle that creates sales over time.

---

### 5. Let Results Arrive Indirectly

Sales often don't come:
- On the first call
- From the expected person
- In the predicted way

But when calls are made from a grounded state, what does show up:
- Referrals
- Information
- Confidence
- Timing alignment
- Unexpected openings

Those are proof not that the tool of cold calling is effective — but rather that your STATE is working the tool. Just like a nail holding two boards together isn't evidence the hammer works — it's evidence the hammer works in that way because the carpenter knows how to use it.
