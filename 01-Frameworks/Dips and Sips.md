# Dips and Sips

## What Resilience Really Feels Like

What does resilience feel like? The word "being resilient" has a positive connotation but when you're being resilient, it definitely doesn't feel positive.

Being resilient doesn't feel resilient at all. It feels so messy.

The space between not knowing something and knowing something is really painful. Especially if you're someone who has fashioned themselves to be academically talented because then it almost feels like your academic success or your identity as a smart person is tied to knowing.

When you're in that in-between space, it's really easy to want to exit something.

This in-between is called the **learning space**. This is what learning, no matter what you're learning, feels like.

What's really empowering to know is that **the learning space has one feeling associated with it: Frustration.**

That's literally how learning feels.

When you think about that, then frustration actually becomes this amazing thing you can learn to love, even though it's painful. Because you actually know that it is a sign that you are learning.

**Whenever you are frustrated, it literally means that you are somewhere within the learning space.**

And why frustration tolerance is so important is because people think resilience and success come from getting to the knowing side as soon as possible. They're wrong.

Resilience and success comes from the length of time you can tolerate being in the learning space. The more you can tolerate being there, the more you will ride it up and down and the more you will eventually get to the knowing.
