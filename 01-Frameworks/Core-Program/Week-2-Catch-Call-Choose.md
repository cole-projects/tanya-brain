## **Week 2: Catch It – Call It Out – Choose Again— Core Takeaways**

---

## **Short Version**

1. You are the awareness observing your thoughts —you’re not the thoughts themselves.That separation \= freedom.  
2. Progress isn’t never thinking negatively — it’s catching it faster.  
3. Awareness weakens old patterns and strengthens the observer within you.  
4. Calling out the thought breaks autopilot and creates choice.  
5. Choosing again builds confidence, identity, and emotional resilience.  
6. Fast, imperfect shifts create more momentum than slow, perfect ones

---

## **Long Version**

## **1\. Awareness Is the Beginning — Repetition Creates Change**

Awareness helps you notice your thoughts.  
But awareness alone does not change them.  
Change happens through:

* Catching the thought  
* Redirecting the thought  
* Repeating the new pathway

The brain learns through repetition, not insight alone.  
The thoughts you practice:

* Become more familiar  
* Become easier to access  
* Become your default

Awareness creates choice.  
Repetition creates transformation.  
---

## **2\. The Brain Will Keep Offering Familiar Thoughts Until You Train It Otherwise**

Your brain is designed to:

* Repeat patterns  
* Default to familiarity  
* Offer habitual thinking

This means:

* Negative thoughts are not failure  
* They are learned pathways

The goal is not to stop thoughts from appearing.  
The goal is:

* To stop automatically believing them  
* To choose differently when they appear

Your power is not in controlling what your mind offers.  
Your power is in what you agree with.  
---

## **3\. The Core Skill: Catch It, Call It Out, Choose Again**

This week introduces the 3-step loop:  
Catch it  
Call it out  
Choose again

Catch it:

* Notice the thought while it’s happening  
* Not hours later  
* Not after you’ve spiraled

Call it out:

* “That’s a thought”  
* “That’s a story”  
* “That’s an interpretation”  
* “My brain is interpreting this as…”  
* “The story I’m telling myself is…”

This creates separation between:

* You  
* And the thought(your brain)

Choose again:

* Reach for a slightly better thought  
* An also valid alternative  
* Believable and also true knowing  
* Not a huge leap  
* Not forced positivity  
* A thought that's useful; energetically helpful

The goal is movement — not perfection.  
---

## **4\. You Cannot Change a Thought You Do Not Notice**

Most thoughts happen:

* Automatically  
* Quickly  
* Below awareness

When you learn to catch them:

* You interrupt the pattern  
* You regain choice  
* You stop automatic spirals

Awareness creates the pause.  
The pause creates freedom.  
---

## **5\. Rewrites Should Be Believable, Not Perfect**

Rewrites fail when:

* They are too big of a leap  
* The nervous system doesn’t feel safe  
* They feel forced or unrealistic

Effective rewrites:

* Feel slightly better  
* Feel believable  
* Create space

You do not need the perfect thought.  
You need a truer, safer next thought.  
Small shifts create:

* Mental flexibility  
* Emotional safety  
* Forward movement

---

## **6\. Practicing Rewrites Builds Cognitive Flexibility**

When you practice:

* You create new neural pathways  
* You build pattern recognition  
* You increase flexibility and fluidity

Without flexibility:

* The mind gets stuck  
* Thoughts loop  
* Emotions intensify

With flexibility:

* You see more options  
* You shift faster  
* You recover quicker

Mental flexibility is more powerful than forced positivity.  
---

## **7\. Rehearsing Ahead of Time Creates Mental Shortcuts**

When you practice common limiting thoughts:

* You build familiarity  
* You prepare your mind  
* You respond faster in real moments

This creates:

* Mental shortcuts that are easiky accessible when triggered  
* Faster recognition  
* Cleaner shifts

Practice when calm so you can access it when stressed.  
---

## **8\. The Observer Self Gets Stronger With Practice**

As you practice:

* You notice thoughts sooner  
* You separate from thoughts more easily  
* You become less reactive

You strengthen:

* The observer self; the watcher  
* The aware version of you  
* The part that chooses instead of reacts

This is where emotional maturity grows.  
---

## **9\. Shifting Your State Improves Your Thinking**

When you’re overwhelmed:

* Fight-or-flight activates  
* Thinking narrows  
* Creativity decreases  
* Your brain defaults to worst-case thinking

When you shift your state:

* The thinking brain re-engages  
* Flexibility increases  
* Clarity improves  
* You see more options

Better state → Better thinking  
Better thinking → Better action  
Better action → Better outcomes  
---

## **10\. Shifting Your State Is Not Avoidance — It’s Preparation**

Rewriting is not:

* Avoiding problems  
* Ignoring reality  
* Escaping discomfort

Rewriting is:

* Becoming calmer  
* Becoming clearer  
* Becoming more capable

You shift your state so you can:

* Solve problems better  
* Reduce reactive decisions  
* Take wiser action

---

## **11\. Small Shifts Create Big Changes**

Even a slight shift:

* Calms the nervous system  
* Expands thinking  
* Opens options

You don’t need dramatic change.  
Small shifts:

* Interrupt spirals  
* Build momentum  
* Improve decisions

A small shift in thought can create a big shift in state.  
---

## **12\. Some Feelings Don’t Need to Be Rewritten**

Not all discomfort needs to be changed.  
Sometimes:

* Sadness is appropriate  
* Grief is appropriate  
* Disappointment is appropriate

The work becomes:

* Allowing the feeling  
* Without layering additional suffering

This prevents:

* Bypassing  
* Forced positivity  
* Emotional suppression

---

## **13\. Rewriting Trains You to Direct Your Mind**

Over time:

* You catch thoughts faster  
* You shift more naturally  
* You spiral less

You become someone who:

* Directs your mind  
* Instead of being directed by it

This builds:

* Emotional resilience  
* Self-trust  
* Inner stability

---

## **14\. Your power is not in never having negative thoughts**

Your power is:

* Catching them sooner  
* Not believing them automatically  
* Choosing again

You don’t need to control your mind.  
You just need to:

* Notice  
* Redirect  
* Repeat

And with practice:  
You become someone who shapes your thinking —  
Instead of being shaped by it.  
---

## **15\. Rehearse Common Thoughts Ahead of Time**

Notice thoughts you say often:

* “This always happens”  
* “I can’t handle this”  
* “I’m behind”  
* “This is too much”

Prepare rewrites ahead of time:

* “This is hard, but I can take one step”  
* “I’ve handled hard things before”  
* “I don’t need to solve everything at once”

Rehearsal creates:

* Faster recognition  
* Faster shifts  
* Less spiraling

---

## **16\. Use Environmental Cues**

Attach awareness to everyday moments:

* When you open your phone  
* When you transition tasks  
* When you feel rushed  
* When you feel stuck

These moments become:

* Awareness checkpoints  
* Pattern interruption opportunities

---

## **17\. The Goal Is Not to Catch Every Thought**

The goal is:

* Catching them sooner  
* Catching them more often  
* Catching them before the spiral deepens

Even catching it:

* 5 minutes sooner  
* 30 seconds sooner  
* One thought sooner

Creates meaningful change.  
---

## **18\. The Most Important Takeaway**

Catching It Sooner Starts With Noticing Your State  
Most thoughts happen below awareness.  
So instead of trying to catch the thought first — you catch the feeling first.  
Your emotional state becomes the signal.  
When you notice:

* Tension  
* Frustration  
* Urgency  
* Overwhelm  
* Discouragement

That’s your cue:  
A thought just happened.  
Feelings are the early warning system.

Ways to Catch It Sooner  
Check in with your state throughout the day:

* “What am I feeling right now?”  
* “What thought might be creating this?”

Use emotional triggers as reminders:

* When you feel contracted  
* When you feel reactive  
* When you feel stuck

That’s your signal to pause and investigate.

You don’t catch thoughts by thinking harder.  
You catch thoughts by becoming more aware of your state.

State → Leads you to the thought  
Thought → Leads you to the rewrite  
Rewrite → Leads you to a new state

And that’s how you start catching it faster and shifting cleaner.

* Awareness is the first step, but awareness alone is not enough — repetition and redirection create change.

* Call it out creates separation by naming it as a thought, story, or interpretation rather than a fact.  
* Choose again means reaching for a slightly better, believable thought — not forcing a huge leap.  
* The goal is not toxic positivity. The goal is to create more space, flexibility, and emotional safety.  
* Practicing with common limiting thoughts helps build pattern recognition, so thoughts become easier to spot.  
* Rehearsing rewrites ahead of time creates mental shortcuts, making it easier to access better thoughts in hard moments.  
* This work strengthens the observer self — the part of you that notices thoughts instead of automatically becoming them.  
* Power pivots and rewrites are not meant to instantly fix life circumstances. They are meant to shift your state so you can respond more clearly.  
* When you are overwhelmed, anxious, or pressured, the brain goes into survival mode, which reduces problem-solving capacity.  
* In fight-or-flight, the brain narrows and defaults to urgency, habit, and worst-case thinking.  
* Even a slight state shift can bring the thinking brain back online, improving creativity, clarity, and decision-making.  
* Shifting your state is not avoidance — it is what helps you solve problems more effectively and with less collateral damage.  
* Practicing rewriting helps train mental flexibility, so the mind learns not to stay locked in one pathway.  
* The point is not to find the perfect thought, but to help the brain move instead of staying frozen or spiraling.  
* Rewrites usually fail when they are too big of a leap, when the nervous system does not feel safe, or when there has not been enough repetition.  
* Some feelings still do not need to be rewritten away. Sometimes sadness is appropriate and the work is to allow it without layering on more suffering.  
* The core takeaway: your power is not in never having negative thoughts — it is in no longer believing them automatically.  
* This week teaches that with practice, you can become someone who directs your mind instead of being directed by it.
