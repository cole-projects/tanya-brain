## **Week 3 The Energy Behind The Words— Core Takeaways**

---

## **Short Version**

1. It’s not just what you say — it’s the energy you say it from.  
2. A rewrite should feel relieving, believable, and grounded — not forced or fake.  
3. Empowering thoughts expand you; bypassing thoughts suppress or pressure you.  
4. The body knows the difference between alignment and avoidance.  
5. The goal isn’t positivity — it’s emotional honesty that gently moves you forward.

## **Long Version**

## **1\. Rewriting Is Not About Saying Positive Words — It’s About Changing Your State**

Rewrites aren’t powerful because of the words themselves.  
They’re powerful because of the energy and nervous system state behind them.

* Bypassing \= forcing positivity to escape discomfort  
* Rewriting \= grounding yourself so you can move through discomfort

---

## **2\. Bypassing Tries to Remove Discomfort. Rewriting Expands Your Capacity to Hold It**

Bypassing says:

* “This shouldn’t be happening”  
* “Make this feeling go away”

Rewriting says:

* “This is hard… and I can still move forward”

One avoids reality.  
The other increases resilience.  
---

## **3\. The Goal of Rewriting Isn’t Just Feeling Better — It’s Moving Differently**

Rewriting matters because:  
When you feel worse:

* You think narrower  
* You act from fear  
* You hesitate or avoid

When you feel better:

* You think clearer  
* You see more possibilities  
* You take more empowered action

Rewrites change your trajectory — not just your mood. They engage a. We directional sequence.   
---

## **4\. Your Nervous System Determines Your Access to Solutions**

When you’re stressed:

* Fight-or-flight activates  
* Prefrontal cortex goes offline  
* Creativity decreases  
* You see fewer options

When you shift your state:

* Your brain re-engages  
* Flexibility increases  
* You see opportunities  
* You make better decisions

Shifting your state isn’t avoiding the problem — it’s becoming better equipped to solve it.  
Better state → Better thinking  
Better thinking → Better action  
Better action → Better outcomes

---

## **5\. The Same Statement Can Be Fear-Based or Truth-Based**

Example:  
“I can handle this”  
Fear-based:

* Tense  
* Defensive  
* Trying to override panic


Truth-based:

* Calm  
* Grounded  
* Trusting your capacity

Same words.  
Different nervous system.  
Different outcome.  
---

## **6\. Bypassing Reduces Agency. Rewriting Increases Agency**

Bypassing:

* Avoids responsibility  
* Seeks relief  
* Hopes things change  
* Intention is to “fix” a moment of discomfort 

Rewriting:

* Increases clarity  
* Increases ownership  
* Moves you toward action  
* Intention is to “transform” the version of you who can hold discomfort differently 

---

## **7\. Rewriting Isn’t Denying Reality — It’s Widening the Lens of Truth**

You’re not pretending things are good.  
You’re choosing:

* The interpretation that gives you power  
* The perspective that opens possibility  
* The thought that supports forward movement

You’re not ignoring reality —  
You’re changing how you show up inside it.  
---

## **8\. You Can Feel the Difference in Your Body**

Fear-Based Rewrites Feel:

* Tight  
* Urgent  
* Forced  
* Defensive

Truth-Based Rewrites Feel:

* Grounded  
* Calm  
* Spacious  
* Steady

Your body becomes the signal for whether you’re bypassing or aligning.  
---

## **9\. The Key Question: Does This Create Pressure or Permission?**

Bypassing creates pressure:

* “I need this to work”  
* “I must stay positive”

Rewriting creates permission:

* “I can handle uncertainty”  
* “I can take one step at a time”

Permission creates movement.  
Pressure creates paralysis.  
---

## **10\. The Most Important Takeaway**

Fear-based rewrites try to control reality.  
Truth-based rewrites create stability within reality.  
And stability is what allows:

* clarity  
* creativity  
* courage  
* aligned action  
* real change

Rewriting isn’t pretending things are okay. It’s becoming the version of you that can handle what’s not.
