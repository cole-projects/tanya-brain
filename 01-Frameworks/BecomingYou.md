# BecomingYou Framework

> This is the source of truth for how transformation actually happens. The purpose of this framework is for clients to understand where they are in their transformation — so they know which tools and concepts apply to their phase, what to focus on, and where they are going next. Each capacity has its own tools. The framework teaches people how to move through change, not just what to do in a moment.

---

## The Core Distinction

**Tools** are situational. They answer: *What should I do right now? What should I think? How do I calm down?*

**The Five Capacities** are structural. They answer: *Where am I in the transformation cycle? What does my nervous system actually need right now? Why is this tool helpful at this moment?*

Without the framework, people use tools randomly. They try to rewrite when they actually need regulation. They try to act when they actually need awareness. They don't know why something isn't working.

With the framework, they understand what phase they're in, they know what kind of work is needed, and they stop forcing the wrong tool at the wrong time.

The tools live inside the capacities. The capacities tell you when to use them.

---

## The Five Capacities

### 1. Circumstance / Contrast / Result — Entry Point of Growth

This is where transformation begins.

A circumstance happens. That circumstance creates contrast. Contrast sparks expansion.

But because this process is cyclical:
- What once felt like contrast becomes the next result
- That result becomes the next circumstance
- Which creates new contrast
- Which sparks more expansion

This is why transformation is iterative and ongoing. Each cycle starts from a higher baseline.

**What this capacity teaches:**
- Circumstances are neutral — the meaning comes from the thought about them, not the circumstance itself
- Contrast is not failure — it is the entry point of growth
- The work here is developing a relationship with contrast, not escaping it
- Expansion begins here

**What comes next:** Awareness — the ability to separate what happened from the story being told about it.

---

### 2. Awareness — Gateway to Change

This is where power begins.

Awareness is the ability to:
- Separate circumstance from thought
- Observe your mind thinking
- Recognize the story being created
- Notice the feeling being generated

This is where someone becomes the observer instead of the reactor.

They begin to notice:
- "I'm thinking this..."
- "My brain is telling this story..."
- "This thought is creating this feeling..."

And in that moment, choice becomes available.

**The critical principle:** Without awareness, all other capacities are inaccessible. You cannot regulate, reorient, act, or allow if you can't first see what's happening.

**What this capacity teaches:**
- You are not your thoughts — you are the one observing them
- Stories are interpretations or assumptions, not facts — the circumstance is neutral, the story is the variable
- Catching limiting thoughts and calling them out is where agency lives — that moment of noticing is the power of choice becoming available
- Choice exists between stimulus and response

**What comes next:** Alignment — stabilizing the energy that awareness has now made visible.

---

### 3. Alignment — Energy Stabilized and Redirected

Alignment is the biggest of all the capacities. It is where energy is stabilized and redirected. It has three sub-phases that must happen in order. Skipping ahead creates bypassing, not transformation.

#### 3A. Emotional Holding — Safety Before Change

Emotional holding is not trying to change how you are feeling. It is simply being with yourself — not trying to shift it, fix it, or move past it.

This is:
- Pausing
- Being with yourself exactly as you are
- Not abandoning yourself in discomfort
- Allowing the feeling without trying to change it
- Your consciousness witnessing your body in a heavy moment

*"I'm here with myself while this is happening."*

This creates safety before change. Without it, any tool applied is bypassing.

#### 3B. Regulation — Active Support

Regulation is still being with yourself — but now actively supporting yourself through it. The goal is to help the nervous system come back to baseline by settling, discharging, or softening activation. This includes anything that grounds the body:
- Breathwork
- Movement
- Music
- Tapping
- Grounding
- Any tool that helps the body feel safer

Emotional holding = stillness with yourself. Regulation = supporting yourself back to baseline. Both create emotional steadiness before re-orientation.

#### 3C. Re-orientation — Where Thinking Begins to Shift

Re-orientation is where identity, energy, and perception begin to shift. It has two parts that build on each other:

**Rewrites** — changing the thought at the root:
- Rewriting thoughts
- Finding relieving perspectives
- Expanding interpretation
- Seeing new possibilities

**Future casting** — rehearsing who is coming:
- Rehearsing future identity
- Practicing future energy
- Casting the version of themselves that has already moved through this

This is where someone becomes self-sourcing, self-accountable, and response-able.

**What this capacity teaches:**
- You cannot rewrite your way out of something your nervous system hasn't been allowed to process
- Emotional holding must come before regulation, regulation before re-orientation
- Tools used out of sequence become bypassing

**What comes next:** Inspired Action — now that perception has shifted, movement feels clearer and more natural.

---

### 4. Inspired Action — Transformation Becomes Visible

Inspired is where transformation becomes visible in the world. This is not forced action — it is movement that arises naturally from the alignment that came before it.

This capacity includes:
- Decisions that feel clear
- Micro-actions taken from a grounded place
- Intentional pause (inaction as a conscious choice)
- Movement toward opportunities
- Following synchronicity
- Taking the next natural step

Because of the previous capacities, movement now feels clearer, easier, and more natural.

This is also where new resources, support, and opportunities become visible (synchronicity), because perception has shifted. They were always there. Now they can be seen.

**What this capacity teaches:**
- Movement from alignment is different from movement taken from fear or force
- Any action that reinforces alignment or identity counts — it does not have to be big
- Small steps from a clear place move faster than big steps from a stuck one
- Synchronicity is not luck — it is the result of aligned perception

**What comes next:** Allowing — and the cycle begins again from a higher baseline.

---

---

### 5. Allowing — Through-Line and Byproduct

Allowing is both the final capacity and the through-line across all capacities.

Allowing includes:
- Trust
- Embodiment
- Identity shift
- Self-reliability

As someone moves through the cycle, they build evidence, confidence, trust, and identity. This creates a feedback loop: trust increases, embodiment increases, movement becomes easier. Then the next circumstance appears, the cycle begins again — but now from a higher baseline.

This is exponential growth. Not because life stops creating contrast, but because each time through the cycle begins from a more solid version of themselves.

**What this capacity teaches:**
- Allowing is not passive — it is the active practice of trusting what you've built
- Even attempting the goal creates wins — the trying itself generates growth, evidence, and momentum
- Identity shifts when you accumulate enough evidence that you are someone who moves through things
- Each cycle doesn't start from zero — it starts from who you became last time

---

## The Deepest Way to Say It

You're not teaching people tools.

You're teaching them:
- How change happens
- How energy moves
- How transformation unfolds
- How identity shifts

So instead of:
*"I need to fix this feeling"*

They begin thinking:
*"I'm in awareness right now... I need emotional holding first... Now I can regulate... Now I can re-orient... Now I can move from an inspired action place... Now I allow."*

They become aware of themselves becoming.

This is a framework for living, not just coaching tools.

---

## How to Use This in a Session

**In coaching responses (70%):** Do not name the framework or the phase out loud. Ask questions that help the client discover where they are themselves.

**Woven into coaching responses (30%):** When a client is naming something real or approaching a shift, briefly introduce a concept or tool that directly applies to what they are going through right now. This is not a lesson — it is one interjected idea, in the moment, that helps them see where they are and what they need next. It reads as one natural sentence inside the coaching response. The client never feels a shift into teaching mode.

The goal is for the client to build their own map over time. Each interjected concept teaches them one more piece of how transformation works — so they increasingly know where they are, what tools belong to their phase, and where they are going next.

**The tool-timing principle:** When a client is trying to force a tool that doesn't fit their phase (e.g., trying to rewrite when they need emotional holding), name what they actually need. This is one of the most valuable things the framework gives — the ability to stop forcing the wrong tool at the wrong time.

**What comes next as a coaching move:** When someone is close to moving through their current phase, weave in a brief note about what's coming — not as a directive, but as illumination. "What's becoming available to you now is..." This is teaching in service of the coaching moment.

## Phase Indicators — What Each Phase Sounds Like

| Phase | What the client says |
|---|---|
| Circumstance / Contrast | "Everything feels like it's falling apart" / "I don't know why this keeps happening" / "I'm so stuck" |
| Awareness | "I'm starting to see the pattern" / "I notice I do this when..." / "I think I know what this is about" |
| Alignment — Emotional Holding | "I just need to sit with this" / "I can't think about it right now" / "I'm overwhelmed" |
| Alignment — Regulation | "I've been doing breathwork" / "I went for a walk" / "I'm trying to calm down" |
| Alignment — Re-orientation | "I'm starting to see it differently" / "What if it's not what I thought?" / "I'm rewriting the story" / "I keep thinking about who I want to become" |
| Inspired Action | "I took the step" / "I reached out" / "I made the decision" / "Something just opened up" / "It felt natural this time" |
| Allowing | "I'm trusting the process" / "I feel different about this now" / "I feel like I'm becoming someone new" |
