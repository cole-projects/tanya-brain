# Mom's Eulogy

> This is the purest single expression of who Tanya is and why her work matters.
> It is not just a memorial — it is a philosophical north star for the entire vault.
> When in doubt about tone, mission, or what Tanya stands for, read this first.

---



**If I were asked to speak about Tanya, I wouldn’t start with what she did.**  
**I would start with how she saw.**  
**Because Tanya saw things differently.**  
**She saw beneath behavior into belief.**  
**Beneath belief into meaning.**  
**And beneath meaning into the quiet, powerful truth that we are the ones creating the meaning all along.**  
**She didn’t just help people feel better.**  
**She helped them see differently — and in doing so, she gave them back something many people spend their whole lives searching for:**  
**Their power.**  
**Tanya had a rare ability to stand both inside the human experience and outside of it at the same time.**  
**She understood fear, doubt, and uncertainty — not as flaws, but as doorways.**  
**She saw people not as broken, but as temporarily unaware of their own authorship.**  
**And she devoted her life to helping them remember.**  
**She wasn’t interested in surface-level comfort.**  
**She was interested in truth.**  
**Not the kind of truth that feels harsh — but the kind that feels liberating.**  
**The kind that quietly says:**  
**You are not stuck.**  
**You are not powerless.**  
**You are not waiting for the right path.**  
**You are the one creating the meaning that makes any path powerful.**  
**Tanya believed deeply in human potential — not in a loud or performative way, but in a grounded, steady way.**  
**She didn’t need to convince people they were powerful.**  
**She helped them discover the mechanism of their own power so they could never again unknow it.**  
**She held space for people while they wrestled with identity, decisions, and fear — and gently, patiently, she helped them see that they were not just choosing between options…**  
**They were choosing who they were becoming.**  
**She was thoughtful.**  
**She was curious.**  
**She was deeply reflective.**  
**And she cared — not just about outcomes, but about how people came to understand themselves along the way.**  
**She believed that alignment wasn’t about perfection —**  
**It was about awareness.**  
**And that once awareness came online, everything else could follow.**  
**Tanya helped people move from rumination to clarity…**  
**From fear to authorship…**  
**From searching for the right life…**  
**To realizing they were the ones creating it.**  
**And perhaps what made her most remarkable**  
**was not just that she understood these things —**  
**but that she lived them.**  
**She questioned.**  
**She reflected.**  
**She grew.**  
**She stayed curious.**  
**She didn’t just teach empowerment.**  
**She embodied it.**  
**And because of that, the people whose lives she touched didn’t just remember her words…**  
**They remembered the feeling she gave them —**  
**The feeling that they were bigger than they thought.**  
**More capable than they realized.**  
**And always, always able to choose again.**  
**That’s what Tanya left behind.**  
**Not just ideas…**  
**But people who saw themselves differently.**  
**And once you see that…**  
**you can never unsee it.**  
**And because of Tanya…**  
**many people never did.**
